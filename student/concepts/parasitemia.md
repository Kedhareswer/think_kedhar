# Dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine

**Therapeutic category:** Antimalarial
**Drug group:** Artemisinin combination therapy (ACT)
**Drug class:** Sesquiterpene endoperoxide + bisquinoline
**Controlled substance:** No

## Overview

Fixed-dose ACT combining dihydroartemisinin (fast-acting blood schizonticide) with piperaquine (long-acting partner drug). Evidence in current corpus covers use as intermittent preventive treatment in pregnancy (IPTp) against [[plasmodium-falciparum]] in endemic settings, where it reduces prevalence of [[parasitemia-in-pregnancy]] versus [[sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine]] [c:da2ba758][c:9eee5181].

## Indication (Why is this medication prescribed?)

- Prevention of [[parasitemia-in-pregnancy]] during 2nd/3rd trimester in HIV-uninfected pregnant adolescents and adults in malaria-endemic regions (Uganda cohort) [c:da2ba758][c:9eee5181] *(pending review for monthly regimen)*.

## Mechanism of Action (How does it work?)

_No mechanism-of-action claims in current corpus._ Claims support clinical effect only — reduced prevalence of parasitemia vs SP comparator [c:da2ba758][c:9eee5181].

Monthly dosing yields lower parasitemia prevalence than three-dose schedule in same trial population.
