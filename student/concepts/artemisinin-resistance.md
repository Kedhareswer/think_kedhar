## Concept Note: Artemisinin Resistance in *Plasmodium falciparum*

**Synthesis of 16 claims | 8 sources | 2026-05-09**

---

### Clinical Significance

Artemisinin resistance reduces ACT effectiveness in malaria-endemic settings (**a3493e90**; moderate certainty, expert opinion). ACTs remain frontline therapy; resistance threatens malaria control and elimination globally.

---

### Primary Molecular Driver: K13 Mutations

Strongest evidence centers on *pfkelch13* (*pfk13*):

| Claim | Mutation | Certainty | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **90d00895** | K13 propeller domain (general) | High | Expert opinion |

| **62c8bfee** | pfk13 (Asia, Africa, S. America) | High | Expert opinion |

K13 propeller mutations are the most replicated, high-certainty causal determinant of artemisinin resistance across global isolates.

---

### Mechanistic Pathway: PI3P → Autophagy → Resistance

Below K13, a coherent mechanistic chain emerges (moderate certainty):

1. **K13 mutation** → impairs ubiquitination of PI3-kinase → **elevated PI3P** (**4b5a733e**)
2. Elevated PI3P → **ER-PI3P vesiculation** (**f657357c**) → activates parasite autophagy
3. **Autophagy pathway activity required** for resistance phenotype (**df6babc2**; Greater Mekong Subregion context)

Experimental support: transgenic PI3-kinase overexpression in *P. falciparum* recapitulates resistance in lab (**8ffb715b**; low certainty, no effect size).

---

### Metabolic Reprogramming (Co-occurring Transcriptomic Signatures)

Meta-analytic evidence (moderate certainty, multiple datasets):

- **Pentose phosphate pathway downregulation** in resistant vs. sensitive isolates (**924620db**)
- **Glycolytic pathway downregulation** in resistant vs. sensitive isolates (**24e63ec9**)

Interpretation: reduced glucose flux may lower hemoglobin digestion and ROS generation, decreasing artemisinin activation—consistent with the "quiescence" model.

---

### Accessory/Background Genetic Markers

Lower-certainty co-occurring alleles identified by GWAS (Southeast Asia context):

| Allele | Claim | Certainty |
|---|---|---|
| arps10-V127M (apicoplast ribosomal protein S10) | **cc48fbc3** | Low |
| fd-D193Y (ferredoxin) | **30af67eb** | Low |
| PfATPase6 variants (sub-Saharan Africa, India) | **f4206d0c** | Very low |

These likely act as **background permissive alleles** that potentiate K13-mediated resistance rather than driving it independently.

---

### Key Gaps

1. No effect-size data on ACT failure rates linked to specific K13 alleles
2. PI3P → autophagy chain established mostly in lab strains; field validation limited
3. African K13 variants (e.g., A675V, C469Y) not represented in these claims despite emerging WHO-validated status
4. Mechanistic role of metabolic downregulation unconfirmed causal direction (cause vs. consequence of resistance)

---

### Bottom Line

K13 propeller mutations are the dominant, well-evidenced cause of *P. falciparum* artemisinin resistance. They act via PI3P elevation → autophagy-mediated proteostasis. Metabolic reprogramming co-occurs consistently. Background alleles (arps10, fd) modulate penetrance. ACT effectiveness is reduced as a downstream consequence. Evidence base is expert-opinion heavy; mechanistic claims need clinical field validation.
