## Concept Note: ART-Induced ER Stress in *P. falciparum*

**Entity:** ART-induced ER stress in *Plasmodium falciparum*
**Source:** PMID:40396048 — *"The ART of Resistance."*

---

### Core Claim

Artemisinin (ART) triggers endoplasmic reticulum (ER) stress in *P. falciparum*, activating unfolded protein response (UPR) → upregulates parasite autophagy.

---

### Mechanistic Path

```
ART exposure
  → protein damage / misfolded protein accumulation
  → ER stress in parasite
  → UPR activation
  → autophagy upregulation
  → [potential resistance / survival mechanism?]
```

---

### Evidence Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|

---

### Significance

UPR-driven autophagy = double-edged: parasite may exploit stress response as **survival/tolerance mechanism** against ART. Relevant to partial resistance phenotype — parasites tolerating ART via proteostatic adaptation rather than target mutation.

---

### Gaps

- No in vivo validation
- Effect size unquantified
- Autophagy upregulation not linked to clinical outcome
- Causal chain UPR→autophagy→resistance not mechanistically closed
- Expert opinion only — needs functional genetic evidence (ATG knockouts, UPR inhibitors)

---

**Bottom line:** Plausible resistance-adjacent mechanism. Moderate confidence. Needs experimental confirmation before clinical inference.
