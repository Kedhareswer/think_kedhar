# Mefloquine

**Therapeutic category:** Antimalarial
**Drug group:** Blood schizonticide
**Drug class:** Quinoline-methanol
**Controlled substance:** No

## Overview

Mefloquine — quinoline-methanol antimalarial active against blood-stage *[[plasmodium-falciparum]]* and *[[plasmodium-vivax]]*. Historically used for chemoprophylaxis, treatment of uncomplicated malaria, and intermittent preventive treatment in pregnancy ([[iptp]]). Resistance and pregnancy-specific risks limit current role.

## Indication (Why is this medication prescribed?)

- Chemoprophylaxis in travelers to chloroquine-resistant zones (background context; no current-corpus claim).
- IPTp considered but **not recommended** vs sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine in second/third trimester, sub-Saharan Africa [c:a0492e3b] (pending review).
- IPTp in HIV-positive pregnant women: avoid; inferior to daily cotrimoxazole regarding mother-to-child HIV transmission risk [c:b25603da] (pending review).

## Mechanism of Action (How does it work?)

Blood schizonticide. Acts on intra-erythrocytic asexual stages of *[[plasmodium-falciparum]]*. Exact target debated — interferes with heme detoxification in parasite food vacuole. *P. falciparum* exhibits resistance, linked to *pfmdr1* amplification [c:b49d0b3a] (pending review).

## Contraindications (When not to use it)

- **IPTp in second/third-trimester pregnancy, sub-Saharan Africa** — avoid vs [[sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine]]; expert opinion [c:a0492e3b] (pending review).
- **HIV-positive pregnant women (2nd/3rd trimester, sub-Saharan Africa)** — avoid as IPTp; raises [[mother-to-child-hiv-transmission]] risk vs [[cotrimoxazole]] daily prophylaxis; meta-analysis [c:b25603da] (pending review).
- Known hypersensitivity (background; no corpus claim).

## Warnings and Precautions
