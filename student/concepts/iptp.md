# IPTp (Intermittent Preventive Treatment in pregnancy)

**Therapeutic category:** Antimalarial prophylaxis strategy
**Drug group:** Intermittent preventive treatment regimen (not a single drug)
**Drug class:** Strategy — varies by partner drug (antifolate, 4-aminoquinoline, ACT)
**Controlled substance:** No

## Overview

IPTp = scheduled curative-dose antimalarial given to pregnant women at antenatal visits in endemic [[sub-saharan-africa]] regardless of parasitaemia. Goal: reduce placental malaria, maternal anaemia, low birth weight. Standard partner drug historically [[sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine]]; alternatives studied where SP resistance high or in [[hiv-coinfection]].

## Indication (Why is this medication prescribed?)

- Prevention of [[malaria-in-pregnancy]] in 2nd/3rd trimester, endemic [[sub-saharan-africa]] [c:32ab180f] (pending review)
- Prevention of malaria in [[hiv-positive-pregnant-women]] on cotrimoxazole prophylaxis [c:747232dc] (pending review, meta-analysis)

## Mechanism of Action (How does it work?)

Scheduled curative dose clears asymptomatic parasitaemia + suppresses new infection during partner-drug half-life. Effect depend on partner drug pharmacology + local resistance.

## Contraindications (When not to use it)

Partner drugs flagged unsuitable as IPTp regimen vs [[sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine]] comparator in 2nd/3rd trimester, endemic sub-Saharan Africa (expert opinion, pending review):

- [[chloroquine]]–[[azithromycin]] — not recommended for IPTp [c:32ab180f]
- [[mefloquine]] — not recommended for IPTp [c:a0492e3b]
- [[amodiaquine]] — not recommended for IPTp [c:f514148b]

Status: relative — reflects comparative tolerability/efficacy vs SP, not absolute pregnancy contraindication.

## Warnings and Precautions

- HIV-positive pregnant women on daily cotrimoxazole: avoid [[sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine]] (sulfa overlap) — use [[dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine]] + cotrimoxazole instead [c:747232dc]
- Mefloquine IPTp linked to tolerability concerns when compared to SP [c:a0492e3b] (pending review)
- Monitor partner-drug resistance trends regionally — efficacy claim setting-bound to sub-Saharan Africa

## Side Effects

## Drug Interactions

- [[cotrimoxazole]] (daily, HIV prophylaxis) + [[sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine]] → additive antifolate / sulfa toxicity; avoid combo — use DHA-PPQ regimen [c:747232dc]
- [[dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine]] + cotrimoxazole: acceptable IPTp regimen in HIV+ on ART (meta-analysis, pending review) [c:747232dc]
