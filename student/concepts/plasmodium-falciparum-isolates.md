## Concept Note: Low CNV Prevalence in *P. falciparum* — Southern Angola

**Entity:** *Plasmodium falciparum* isolates
**Source:** PMID:39794826 | Lubango, southern Angola | Hospital, endemic setting

---

### Core Finding

Two resistance-associated copy number variants (CNVs) screened in *P. falciparum* isolates from Lubango:

| Gene | CNV Type | Prevalence |
|------|----------|------------|
| *pfmdr1* | Increased copy number | **8.9%** |
| *pfpm2* | Increased copy number | **9.8%** |

Both rates low. Pattern consistent with study title: *"Low prevalence of CNV in pfmdr1 and pfpm2."*

---

### Clinical Relevance

- **pfmdr1** amplification → reduced sensitivity to **lumefantrine** (artemether-lumefantrine partner drug); also linked to **mefloquine** resistance
- **pfpm2** amplification → **piperaquine** resistance (dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine failure)
- Low prevalence suggests current first-line regimens retain efficacy in this population — but not zero pressure

---

### Caveats

- Evidence grade: **expert_opinion** (not RCT/observational cohort) — certainty **moderate**
- No CIs reported → precision of 8.9%/9.8% estimates unknown
- Hospital-only sample → may not reflect community-wide parasite pool
- Both claims **pending_review**

---

### Synthesis

Angola *P. falciparum* isolates in Lubango show low but non-negligible CNV in both *pfmdr1* and *pfpm2* (~9% each). No dominant amplification signal. Continued molecular surveillance warranted as artemisinin-based combination therapy scales.
