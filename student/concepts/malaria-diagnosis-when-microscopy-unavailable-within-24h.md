# Rapid Diagnostic Test (Malaria RDT)

**Therapeutic category:** Diagnostic device (point-of-care)
**Drug group:** Immunochromatographic lateral-flow assay
**Drug class:** Antigen-capture rapid test (HRP-2 / pLDH targets)
**Controlled substance:** No

## Overview

Malaria RDT is a point-of-care lateral-flow immunoassay detecting *Plasmodium* antigens from a fingerprick blood sample. Used to confirm malaria when [[microscopy]] unavailable within 24 hours in endemic settings. Result in 15–20 minutes. Note: this concept note covers a **diagnostic recommendation**, not a therapeutic drug — dosing/pharmacology sections not applicable.

## Indication (Why is this medication prescribed?)

- Diagnosis of [[malaria]] in patients with fever in endemic regions of [[india]] when microscopy result not obtainable within 24 h [c:cdca1173] [c:438211ce] *(pending review, expert_opinion)*
- Comparator standard: [[microscopy]] (preferred when available) [c:cdca1173]

## Mechanism of Action (How does it work?)

Antibody-coated nitrocellulose strip captures circulating *Plasmodium* antigens (HRP-2 for *P. falciparum*, pLDH for pan-species) from whole blood. Antigen–antibody complex migrates via capillary action, binds labeled conjugate, produces visible band. Not a pharmacologic agent — no MoA in drug sense. Diagnostic role specified by claim set [c:cdca1173].
