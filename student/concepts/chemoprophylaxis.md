## Overview

Chemoprophylaxis = scheduled antimalarial dosing before, during, after travel to endemic zone. Prevents [[malaria]] in non-immune travelers. Not single drug — covers atovaquone-proguanil, doxycycline, mefloquine, chloroquine, primaquine, tafenoquine. Specific agent picked by destination resistance pattern, traveler comorbidities, pregnancy status. ## Mechanism of Action (How does it work?)

Agent-dependent. Schizonticidal agents (atovaquone-proguanil, doxycycline, mefloquine, chloroquine) suppress erythrocytic stage of [[plasmodium-falciparum]] and other species. Hypnozoiticidal agents (primaquine, tafenoquine) kill liver-stage hypnozoites of [[plasmodium-vivax]] / [[plasmodium-ovale]] — true causal prophylaxis. _No mechanism claims in current corpus — see individual drug notes._
