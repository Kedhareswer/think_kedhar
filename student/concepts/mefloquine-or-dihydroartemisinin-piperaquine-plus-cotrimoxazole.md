# Mefloquine or Dihydroartemisinin-Piperaquine plus Cotrimoxazole

**Therapeutic category:** Antimalarial (intermittent preventive treatment in pregnancy, IPTp)
**Drug group:** Combination IPTp regimen — quinoline-methanol or artemisinin-combination therapy + folate-pathway antibiotic
**Drug class:** Mixed (mefloquine = 4-quinolinemethanol; dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine = artemisinin + bisquinoline; cotrimoxazole = sulfonamide/diaminopyrimidine)
**Controlled substance:** No

## Overview

Combination regimen for [[malaria-prevention-in-pregnancy]] in HIV-positive women in sub-Saharan Africa. Adds either mefloquine or dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine to daily cotrimoxazole prophylaxis during 2nd/3rd trimester. Targets [[placental-malaria]] and maternal parasitaemia where standard sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine IPTp contraindicated by cotrimoxazole co-administration.

## Indication (Why is this medication prescribed?)

- Prevention of [[placental-malaria]] in HIV-positive pregnant adults, 2nd/3rd trimester, sub-Saharan Africa, on daily cotrimoxazole [c:b23da375] (pending review)
- Prevention of maternal peripheral parasitaemia at delivery, same population [c:1293ab5b] (pending review)

## Mechanism of Action (How does it work?)

Combination acts on multiple [[plasmodium-falciparum]] stages. [[Mefloquine]] and [[piperaquine]] = blood schizonticides disrupting heme detoxification in parasite digestive vacuole. [[Dihydroartemisinin]] = endoperoxide bridge activated by heme-iron, generates radicals damaging parasite proteins. [[Cotrimoxazole]] ([[trimethoprim]] + [[sulfamethoxazole]]) inhibits folate synthesis (DHPS) and reduction (DHFR), providing background suppression.
