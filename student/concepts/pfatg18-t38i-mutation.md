## Concept Note — PfATG18 T38I Mutation

**Entity:** PfATG18 T38I | *Plasmodium falciparum* autophagy-related protein 18, threonine→isoleucine at residue 38

---

### Summary

PfATG18 T38I is a point mutation in *P. falciparum* ATG18 — a phosphoinositide-binding protein involved in autophagy and artemisinin (ART) stress-response pathways. In field isolates across South-east Asia, T38I co-occurs with multiple PfKelch13 propeller mutations associated with ART partial resistance.

---

### Co-occurrence Evidence

All three claims originate from a single source (PMID:40396048, *"The ART of Resistance"*), classified as expert opinion, moderate certainty, pending review.

| Kelch13 Allele | Region | Setting | Evidence grade |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Y493H** | South-east Asia | Field / endemic | Expert opinion |
| **R539T** | South-east Asia | Field / endemic | Expert opinion |
| **C580Y** | South-east Asia | Field / endemic | Expert opinion |

No quantitative effect sizes, confidence intervals, or allele frequencies reported in current records.

---

### Interpretation

- T38I observed alongside **three distinct validated Kelch13 resistance alleles** → likely a co-selected background mutation, not allele-specific.
- PfATG18 role in autophagy/PI3P binding suggests T38I may modulate parasite stress responses that intersect with ART-induced damage clearance — plausible mechanistic link to resistance background.
- Absence of experimental effect-size data limits causal inference; current status = associative / epidemiological.
