## Concept Note: Paclitaxel

**Entity:** Paclitaxel
**Source:** [PMID:42059554](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42059554/)
**Status:** Pending review | Evidence: Expert opinion | Certainty: Low

---

### Mechanism

Paclitaxel disrupts *P. falciparum* β-tubulin via M-loop flexibility changes and global structural compaction (in silico). Tubulin dysfunction stalls parasite development.

### Therapeutic Signal

Synergistic potential with Dihydroartemisinin (DHA) against artemisinin-resistant strains. Combination exploits distinct mechanism — tubulin stabilisation vs. artemisinin oxidative stress — to overcome resistance.

### Evidence Summary

| Claim | Setting | Certainty | Grade |
|-------|---------|-----------|-------|
| β-tubulin structural perturbation | In silico | Low | Expert opinion |
| Combination with DHA for resistant malaria | — | Low | Expert opinion |

### Caveats

- Both claims from single source; no in vitro / in vivo validation cited
- No dose, regimen, or population data
- Paclitaxel has significant mammalian toxicity — therapeutic window for antiparasitic use uncharacterised
- Claims `7b9136e0` and `2f6c2614` both **pending review**

### Bottom Line

Computational signal only. Paclitaxel–DHA combo warrants wet-lab validation before any clinical consideration. Resistance-mechanism angle is novel; evidence base too thin to act on.
