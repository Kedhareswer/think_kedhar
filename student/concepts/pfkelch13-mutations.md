## Concept Note: PfKelch13 Mutations & Artemisinin Resistance

**Entity:** `pfkelch13 mutations` | **Date:** 2026-05-09 | **Claims:** 3 | **Sources:** 2 PMIDs

---

### Core Assertion

PfKelch13 mutations **cause** clinical artemisinin (ART) resistance in *P. falciparum* (certainty: **high**). Specific variants C580Y, R539T, Y493H confirmed in Southeast Asian field isolates vs. wild-type comparator.

---

### Mechanistic Context

Resistance likely operates via **convergent heme limitation**: PfKelch13 mutations co-occur with PfCoronin mutations in a shared mechanism that restricts heme availability. ART activation depends on heme-mediated cleavage of its endoperoxide bridge — less free heme → less activated drug → parasite survives.

| Partner | Mechanism | Certainty |
|---|---|---|
| PfKelch13 alone | Reduces heme flux, slows ring-stage development | High |
| PfCoronin + PfKelch13 | Convergent heme restriction, additive resistance | Low |

Mechanistic evidence points to heme availability as the convergent node, with PfCoronin as a potential epistatic partner — but that interaction remains hypothesis-level. Claims sourced from 2 papers (one review, one mechanistic study), both graded expert opinion; formal evidence upgrade needed before clinical guideline use.
