## Artemisinins — Concept Note

**Role:** Artemisinins are first-line agents for *Plasmodium falciparum* malaria globally (claim 907989a6; PMID:25874676, high certainty).

**Resistance threat:** *P. falciparum* strains in eastern Africa and southeast Asia resist artemisinins (claim fc5e75f7; PMID:38552654, high certainty). Primary molecular driver: PfKelch13 C580Y mutation vs. wild-type (claim bd39741a; PMID:25874676, high certainty). C580Y disrupts artemisinin's mechanism of action, conferring partial resistance in endemic settings.

**Next-generation options:** Synthetic ozonides — artefenomel (OZ439) and OZ277 — show longer in vivo half-life than artemisinins, positioning them as potential successors (claim d3b9b766; PMID:41959371, moderate certainty). Notably, double PfKelch13 mutations also confer resistance to these next-gen derivatives, limiting their promise.

**Evidence gaps:** No quantitative effect sizes (no ORs, RRs, or CIs) across any claim. Population specifics (age, pregnancy status, immune status) absent. Ozonide claim is moderate certainty only — pipeline stage unclear.

**Bottom line:** Artemisinins remain global standard; resistance expanding via PfKelch13 mutations in two continents; ozonide successors exist but face same mutation-driven resistance. Urgent need for quantitative resistance surveillance and novel mechanism drugs.
