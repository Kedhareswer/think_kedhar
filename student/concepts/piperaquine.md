# Piperaquine

**Therapeutic category:** Antimalarial
**Drug group:** Bisquinoline (partner drug in ACT)
**Drug class:** 4-aminoquinoline dimer
**Controlled substance:** No

## Overview

Piperaquine — long-acting bisquinoline antimalarial. Paired with [[dihydroartemisinin]] as ACT for [[uncomplicated-falciparum-malaria]] treatment and intermittent preventive treatment in pregnancy (IPTp). Long half-life gives post-treatment prophylaxis but raises cardiac and resistance concerns.

## Indication (Why is this medication prescribed?)

- Treatment of [[uncomplicated-falciparum-malaria]] (combined with [[dihydroartemisinin]]).
- Intermittent preventive treatment in pregnancy (IPTp) in [[plasmodium-falciparum]] endemic areas, second/third trimester [c:53b10243] _(pending review)_.

## Mechanism of Action (How does it work?)

Bisquinoline. Concentrate in parasite digestive vacuole. Block heme detoxification → toxic heme accumulate → parasite death. Long elimination half-life (~3 weeks) extend post-treatment prophylaxis.

## Warnings and Precautions

- **QT interval prolongation** — concentration-dependent. Documented in HIV-uninfected pregnant adults (2nd/3rd trimester) on outpatient IPTp in endemic settings [c:53b10243] _(pending review, RCT)_. Monitor ECG / avoid co-administered QT-prolonging agents.
- **Resistance** — [[plasmodium-falciparum]] resistance to piperaquine documented [c:dc079267] _(pending review, expert_opinion)_. Surveillance required; consider local efficacy data before deployment.
