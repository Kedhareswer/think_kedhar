## Dihydroartemisinin-Piperaquine (DHA-PPQ) — Concept Note

**Drug class:** Artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT). Paired fast-acting DHA with long half-life piperaquine.

---

### Efficacy

High-certainty evidence from Uganda (3 sentinel sites, outpatient, children 6 mo–10 yr): PCR-corrected 28-day cumulative efficacy **98.9–100%** vs. artemether-lumefantrine (AL) for uncomplicated *P. falciparum* malaria. Efficacy essentially equivalent to AL in this African context. *(PMID:34952573)*

---

### Safety

Same Uganda trial: serious adverse event rate = **0.0** in DHA-PPQ arm vs. AL comparator. Moderate certainty (expert opinion grade). No serious safety signal in pediatric outpatient population. *(PMID:34952573)*

---

### Resistance

High-certainty signal from Southeast Asia: Cambodian *P. falciparum* strains show **resistance to DHA-PPQ**, persisting despite the "plasmepsin-piperaquine paradox" (plasmepsin 2/3 amplification mechanism under ongoing investigation). Drug-sensitive strains remain the comparator. This resistance is geographically restricted but a major containment concern. *(PMID:40720544)*

---

### Synthesis

DHA-PPQ = highly effective, well-tolerated ACT for African pediatric malaria. Resistance confined to SEA (Cambodia-origin strains) with no current Uganda signal — but regional monitoring critical given documented resistance mechanisms. Evidence grade uniformly tagged `expert_opinion`; RCT-level re-grading warranted given trial design of PMID:34952573.
