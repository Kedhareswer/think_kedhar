## Concept Note: dihydroartemisinin IC50

**Entity:** dihydroartemisinin IC50
**Source:** PMID:39545737 | Eastern & northern Uganda, endemic P. falciparum

---

### What it is

DHA IC50 = in vitro inhibitory concentration measuring artemisinin potency against P. falciparum ring-stage parasites. Used here as cross-validation anchor for two artemisinin resistance phenotyping assays.

---

### Key relationships (both claims, same source)

| Assay metric | Direction | Spearman rs | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **eRRSA fold change** | Negative | −0.412 | Higher IC50 → smaller recovery fold change → more resistant parasites tolerate DHA |
| **RSA % survival** | Positive | +0.424 | Higher IC50 → more parasites survive → resistance signal aligns |

Both correlations modest-to-moderate strength, consistent direction: higher DHA IC50 tracks with both resistance readouts.

---

### Mechanistic logic

IC50 (growth inhibition) and survival assays (ring-stage tolerance) measure related but distinct resistance axes. Convergent correlation with DHA IC50 validates eRRSA as functionally equivalent to RSA for field isolate phenotyping.

---

### Caveats

- Evidence grade: `expert_opinion` (not RCT/observational cohort)
- Status: `pending_review`
- No CI on rs values — precision unknown
- Single geographic region (Uganda) — generalizability unconfirmed
- Correlation ≠ causal pathway; confounders (parasite genetics, mixed infections) not adjusted

---

### Bottom line

DHA IC50 weakly-moderately correlated with both RSA and eRRSA resistance readouts in Ugandan P. falciparum. Supports eRRSA as scalable RSA alternative. IC50 alone insufficient — resistance phenotyping needs survival assay confirmation.
