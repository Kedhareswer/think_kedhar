## Concept Note: *Plasmodium falciparum* K13 R539T+A212T Double Mutation

**Entity:** Plasmodium falciparum Kelch13 (K13) double mutant — R539T + A212T
**Source:** PMID:41959371 | *Double Mutations in Plasmodium falciparum Kelch13 drive resistance to next-generation artemisinin derivatives in malaria parasites*
**Date synthesized:** 2026-05-09

---

### Summary

K13 R539T+A212T double mutant *P. falciparum* shows enhanced resistance to **both clinically advanced next-generation synthetic ozonide antimalarials** — OZ277 (arterolane) and OZ439 (artefenomel) — compared to the R539T single mutant. Both resistance phenotypes are documented in Cambodian malaria-endemic field context.

---

### Key Points

**Mechanistic context.** K13 mutations impair ubiquitin/proteasome-mediated hemoglobin digestion, reducing activation of endoperoxide antimalarials. The A212T secondary mutation compounds resistance likely by further destabilizing K13-dependent drug activation or altering parasite stress-response kinetics.

**Clinical significance.** OZ277 and OZ439 were developed specifically as artemisinin replacements against ART-resistant parasites. Resistance emerging in *double* K13 mutants narrows the replacement pipeline. OZ439 resistance reaching **high certainty** is the more urgent signal.

**Geographic risk.** Both claims localized to **Cambodia** — ground zero for ART resistance emergence and spread. Historical pattern suggests regional spread to GMS and potentially sub-Saharan Africa over years.

**Evidence gap.** Evidence grade is **expert opinion** for both; no quantitative IC₅₀ shift, RSA survival rates, or in vivo pharmacokinetic data captured in these claims. Effect size metrics are null — limits precision of resistance magnitude assessment.

---

### Uncertainties & Gaps

- No dose-response data (IC₅₀ fold-change vs. wildtype or R539T single mutant)
- No RSA (Ring-stage Survival Assay) % values
- Fitness cost of double mutation not reported
- Transmission competence of double mutant unknown
- Whether A212T alone confers resistance unresolved

### Implications

1. **Surveillance priority:** Monitor A212T co-occurrence with established K13 mutations across GMS and Africa
2. **Pipeline alert:** OZ439 Phase II/III programs should include double-mutant susceptibility data
3. **Combination strategy:** Ozonide monotherapy increasingly untenable in Cambodia; partner drug optimization urgent
4. **Evidence upgrade needed:** Experimental RSA + IC₅₀ data required to convert expert opinion → experimental evidence grade
