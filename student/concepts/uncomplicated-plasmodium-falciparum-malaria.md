## Concept Note: Uncomplicated *Plasmodium falciparum* Malaria — Treatment Efficacy

**Entity:** Uncomplicated *Plasmodium falciparum* malaria
**Claims synthesized:** 4 | **Sources:** 3 PMIDs | **Date:** 2026-05-09

---

### Core Treatment Landscape

Artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT) is established first-line treatment for uncomplicated *P. falciparum* malaria in outpatient settings (PMID:38321292, certainty: **high**). Across malaria-endemic sub-Saharan Africa, artemisinin-based regimens remain the dominant therapeutic backbone (PMID:38552654, certainty: **high**).

---

### Head-to-Head Efficacy (Uganda, Children 6 mo–10 yr)

Both standard ACT regimens effective; DHA-PPQ outperforms AL:

| Regimen | PCR-corrected 28-day efficacy (95% CI) | Certainty |
|---|---|---|
| Dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DHA-PPQ) | 98.9–100.0% | **High** |
| Artemether-lumefantrine (AL) | 87.2–94.4% | Moderate |

Source: PMID:34952573 — outpatient RCT, three Ugandan sentinel sites.

---

### Key Observations

- **DHA-PPQ superiority** in this cohort likely reflects residual AL susceptibility erosion; piperaquine's longer half-life confers extended post-treatment prophylaxis.
- **AL efficacy range** (87–94%) approaches WHO acceptability floor (≥90%); flags need for local sentinel surveillance before defaulting to AL.
- **Resistance pressure** context: two sources (PMID:38321292, PMID:38552654) flag artemisinin partial resistance emerging in Africa — efficacy data above predates or captures early phase; interpret DHA-PPQ figures with this trajectory in mind.
- All four claims tagged `evidence_grade: expert_opinion` despite RCT source — review warranted before clinical decision integration.
