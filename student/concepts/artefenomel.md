## Concept Note: Artefenomel (OZ439) — Resistance via K13 Double Mutations

**Entity:** artefenomel (OZ439)  
**Source:** PMID:41959371 | *Double Mutations in Plasmodium falciparum Kelch13 drive resistance to next-generation artemisinin derivatives in malaria parasites.*  
**Date synthesized:** 2026-05-09

---

### Summary

Two *Plasmodium falciparum* K13 double mutations confer resistance to artefenomel beyond what single mutations alone produce. Both implicate A212T as a resistance-amplifying second hit.

---

### Claims

| # | Subject | Comparator | Certainty | Evidence grade |
|---|---------|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | K13 **C580Y + A212T** | C580Y single mutant | Moderate | Expert opinion |
| 2 | K13 **R539T + A212T** | R539T single mutant | High | Uncertain |

- **Region:** Cambodia (malaria-endemic)  
- **Effect size:** Not quantified in extracted data (metric/CI absent)  
- **Status:** Both pending review

---

### Synthesis

**A212T acts as resistance amplifier.** Both claims share A212T as second mutation. Pattern suggests A212T potentiates existing K13 mutations (C580Y, R539T) against OZ439, not just standard artemisinins. Mechanism likely same piperaquine-independent K13-mediated ring-stage survival pathway, extended to synthetic peroxide scaffold.

**Clinical relevance — Cambodia:** C580Y already dominant in SEA. C580Y+A212T double mutants emerging = threat to OZ439 pipeline as partner drug or monotherapy. R539T less prevalent but R539T+A212T shows *higher* certainty resistance, suggesting stronger phenotypic effect.

**Evidence gap:** No IC50, RSA survival rates, or clinical failure data extracted. Expert opinion grade limits actionability. Quantitative resistance metrics needed.
