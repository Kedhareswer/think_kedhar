## Concept Note: *P. falciparum* K13 C580Y+A212T Double Mutation

**Entity:** *Plasmodium falciparum* Kelch13 C580Y+A212T double mutant
**Date:** 2026-05-09 | **Source:** PMID:41959371

---

### Summary

K13 C580Y is dominant artemisinin-resistance mutation in Southeast Asia. A212T second-site mutation in Kelch13 confers additive resistance to next-generation synthetic ozonide antimalarials beyond C580Y alone.

---

### Resistance Profile

| Drug | Class | Comparator | Certainty | Evidence |
|------|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| OZ277 (arterolane) | Ozonide | K13 C580Y single mutant | Moderate | Expert opinion |
| OZ439 (artefenomel) | Ozonide | K13 C580Y single mutant | Moderate | Expert opinion |

Both claims pending review. No quantitative IC50/RSA effect sizes reported.

---

### Key Points

- **Context:** Cambodia, malaria-endemic. Both OZ compounds designed as ART-resistance-evasion candidates.
- **Significance:** Double mutant undermines ozonide pipeline meant to replace failing artemisinins. C580Y+A212T = resistance expansion beyond first-gen ART.
- **Evidence gap:** Effect sizes (metric, CI) null — mechanism/magnitude unquantified in source. Expert opinion grade limits confidence.
- **Clinical implication:** Partner drug reliance critical. Ozonide monotherapy inadequate against double-mutant background.
