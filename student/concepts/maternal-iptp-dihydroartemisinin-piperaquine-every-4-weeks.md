# Dihydroartemisinin-Piperaquine (maternal IPTp, every 4 weeks)

**Therapeutic category:** Antimalarial
**Drug group:** Artemisinin combination therapy
**Drug class:** Sesquiterpene endoperoxide + bisquinoline
**Controlled substance:** No

## Overview

Dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DP) given every 4 weeks during pregnancy as intermittent preventive treatment (IPTp) in malaria-endemic settings. Compared with standard IPTp-sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine (SP), the more intensive DP regimen is under evaluation for downstream effects on offspring malaria risk in infancy [c:e2b08cd0] (pending review).

## Warnings and Precautions

- **Offspring malaria risk signal.** Maternal IPTp-DP q4w vs IPTp-SP q8w → ↑ overall malaria incidence in offspring <2 yr; aIRR 1.92 (95% CI 1.00–3.65), [[tororo-uganda]], endemic, malaria-naive infants [c:e2b08cd0], RCT, low certainty (pending review).
- **Sex-specific signal in female offspring.** aIRR 4.39 (95% CI 1.87–10.3) for female children <2 yr under maternal q4w DP + infant DP q12w regimen [c:220e6cef], RCT, low certainty (pending review).
- Both signals from single-site RCT, wide CIs, low certainty. Do not generalize beyond [[tororo-uganda]]-like endemic settings without confirmation.
