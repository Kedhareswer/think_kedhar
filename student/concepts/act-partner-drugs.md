# ACT Partner Drugs

**Therapeutic category:** Antimalarial
**Drug group:** Artemisinin-combination therapy (partner component)
**Drug class:** Heterogeneous — quinoline/aminoalcohol partners paired with artemisinin derivative
**Controlled substance:** No

## Overview

ACT partner drugs are the longer-acting antimalarials co-formulated with a short-acting artemisinin derivative in [[artemisinin-combination-therapy]]. The artemisinin clears the bulk parasite burden; the partner eliminates residual parasites and suppresses recrudescence. Common partners include [[lumefantrine]], [[amodiaquine]], [[mefloquine]], [[piperaquine]], and [[pyronaridine]]. Selection depends on local resistance patterns of [[plasmodium-falciparum]].
