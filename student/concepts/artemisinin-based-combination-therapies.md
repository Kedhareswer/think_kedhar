## Concept Note: Artemisinin-Based Combination Therapies (ACTs)

**Entity:** Artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs)
**Synthesized:** 2026-05-09 | Claims: 4 | Sources: 3 PMIDs

---

### Role

ACTs = first-line outpatient treatment for uncomplicated *P. falciparum* malaria globally (high certainty; expert opinion — PMID:34267045).

---

### Resistance Signal

All 3 remaining claims converge on one threat: **artemisinin-resistant *P. falciparum* undermines ACT efficacy**.

| Mechanism | Geography | Evidence grade | Certainty | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| *kelch13* (K13) mutations | SE Asia + Uganda | Cohort (modelling) | High | PMID:40112841 |
| Partial artemisinin resistance (phenotype) | Kenya, Peru, Thailand | Cohort (multicenter RCT-style) | High | PMID:40614930 |
| Multidrug-resistant *Pf* (incl. partner drug) | Greater Mekong Subregion | Expert opinion | High | PMID:34267045 |

Key pattern: resistance originated in **Greater Mekong Subregion** (K13 + partner drug resistance), now detected in **Africa** (Uganda) via modelling, and confirmed phenotypically across **3 continents** (Kenya/Peru/Thailand multicenter trial).

---

### Synthesis

ACTs remain the backbone of falciparum malaria treatment, but face a high-certainty, geographically expanding resistance threat driven by K13 mutations and co-evolving partner-drug resistance. Uganda emergence signals that the African reservoir — where most malaria burden lies — is no longer insulated. The Kenya/Peru/Thailand multicenter data extend resistance documentation beyond Asia into Africa and South America.

**Critical gap:** No claims capture effect size metrics (cure rates, day-3 positivity, HR/OR). All `effect_size` fields null. Quantitative impact on treatment failure rates unrepresented in current claim set.
