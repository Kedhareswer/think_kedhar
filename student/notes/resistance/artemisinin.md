## Artemisinin Resistance in *P. falciparum* — Synthesized Note

### Core Mechanism: Kelch13 (K13) Mutations

Primary resistance driver. Mutations in *pfkelch13* propeller domain reduce artemisinin activity during ring stage. Mechanism involves autophagy-mediated proteostasis (PMID:35420484) and altered fatty acid metabolism (PMID:37385107).

**Validated mutations by region:**

| Mutation | Region | Certainty | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| C580Y | Greater Mekong Subregion | High | Expert opinion |
| R561H | Tanzania (Kagera, NW) | High | Expert opinion |
| 476I, 539T, 561H | sub-Saharan Africa + India | Moderate | Meta-analysis |
| General K13 | 15 countries (MalariaGEN) | High | Meta-analysis |

**C580Y** is best-characterized: validated in isogenic lines vs. wild-type PfK13; multi-omics shows ring-stage fatty acid insensitivity to ART (PMID:37385107). **R561H** confirmed in Tanzanian children 6–120 months (dispensary-level surveillance; PMID:39159633) — signals Africa spread.

### Secondary Mechanism: Pfcoronin Mutations

Pfcoronin (G50E, R100K, E107V) associated with resistance in Africa. **Low certainty** for these specific variants (PMID:34452235). Broader pfcoronin mutations supported at moderate certainty with clearer cellular/molecular basis emerging (PMID:40964400). K13-independent pathway — relevant for non-GMS contexts.

### Detection

Ring-stage survival assay (RSA) is standard in-vitro correlate. Extended recovery RSA (erRSA) superior predictor of *in vivo* patient clearance half-life vs. standard 6h DHA exposure (PMID:32005233). Flow cytometry-based RSA enables higher throughput (PMID:24867976).

### Epidemiology Signal

- GMS: entrenched K13 resistance, C580Y dominant
- Africa: K13 variants (R561H Tanzania confirmed; 476I/539T/561H circulating sub-Saharan + India) — partial resistance, not yet full treatment failure
- Modeling: ACT combination strategy critical to slow spread (PMID:26545449)

### Certainty Landscape

| Claim type | Certainty | Grade |
|---|---|---|
| K13 → resistance (GMS, Africa, India) | High | Expert opinion / Meta-analysis |
| C580Y mechanism (autophagy, fatty acid) | Moderate–High | Expert opinion |

### Gaps

Most claims lack quantitative effect sizes (no HR/OR/CI). Pfcoronin claims weak — need clinical correlation. Africa K13 spread trajectory unquantified. No pregnancy-specific data across any claim.
