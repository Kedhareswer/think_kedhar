## Topic Note: Resistance / Artemisinin-Based Combination Therapies
*Synthesized 2026-05-09 · 2 claims · evidence grade: cohort · certainty: high*

---

### Core Finding

*P. falciparum* kelch13 (k13) mutations drive partial artemisinin resistance → reduced ACT efficacy. No longer confined to SE Asia — now documented in Uganda (Africa) and confirmed across Kenya, Peru, Thailand via multicenter trial.

---

### Evidence Summary

| Claim | Subject | Region | Source |
|-------|---------|--------|--------|
| 1034b458 | k13 mutations selected under ACT pressure | SE Asia, Uganda | PMID:40112841 (modelling + cohort) |
| 70388c43 | Artemisinin-resistant *Pf* resists ACTs | Kenya, Peru, Thailand | PMID:40614930 (multicenter trial) |

---

### Key Points

- **Mechanism**: k13 mutations (e.g., R539T, C580Y) impair artemisinin binding → slower parasite clearance → ACT failure risk
- **Geographic spread**: resistance no longer SE Asia–exclusive; Africa (Uganda) + Americas (Peru) + Asia confirmed
- **Modelling (PMID:40112841)**: Ugandan k13 selection dynamics mirror SE Asian emergence pattern — suggests same evolutionary pressure from ACT use
- **Clinical trial (PMID:40614930)**: global partial resistance confirmed in uncomplicated *Pf* malaria; "partial" = delayed clearance, not full treatment failure, but partner-drug resistance amplifies risk

---

### Clinical Implications

- ACT efficacy contingent on **partner drug** (lumefantrine, piperaquine, amodiaquine) — if partner drug also compromised, failure probable
- Routine k13 surveillance warranted in Africa, not just SE Asia
- Treatment guidelines may need region-specific ACT selection based on local k13 prevalence

---

### Gaps / Caveats

- Effect sizes (clearance t½, day-3 positivity rates) not captured in claims — need extraction
- Pregnancy and pediatric subgroup data absent
- "Partial" resistance clinical threshold not standardized across trials
- Peru finding notable — implies independent emergence or importation in low-transmission setting
