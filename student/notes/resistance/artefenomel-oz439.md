## Topic Note: Resistance — Artefenomel (OZ439)

**Source:** PMID:41959371 · *Double Mutations in P. falciparum Kelch13 drive resistance to next-generation artemisinin derivatives*

---

### Core Finding

K13 **double mutations** (canonical resistance allele + A212T) confer enhanced resistance to artefenomel (OZ439) compared to single-mutant backgrounds. Two double-mutant combinations implicated:

| Double Mutant | Comparator (baseline) | Certainty |
|---|---|---|
| **C580Y + A212T** | C580Y single mutant | Moderate |
| **R539T + A212T** | R539T single mutant | High |

A212T appears to act as an **additive/synergistic second hit** on top of established Southeast Asian resistance alleles.

---

### Context

- **Region:** Cambodia (malaria-endemic)
- **Drug class:** Ozonide / synthetic peroxide — next-gen ART derivative developed partly to overcome K13-mediated artemisinin resistance
- **Evidence grade:** Expert opinion (both claims) — likely in vitro / molecular modeling; no clinical trial data yet

---

### Implications

- OZ439 was positioned as a successor to combat K13-resistant *Pf*. These double mutations suggest **resistance evolution can track onto ozonides**, not just ACTs.
- A212T pairing with multiple primary K13 alleles (C580Y, R539T) raises concern for a **generalizable resistance scaffold** rather than an isolated event.
- Effect size metrics absent — magnitude of RSA shift or IC₅₀ increase vs. single mutants not yet captured in these claims; needs extraction or follow-up.
