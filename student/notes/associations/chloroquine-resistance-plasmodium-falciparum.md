## Topic Note: Chloroquine Resistance — *Plasmodium falciparum*

**Source:** PMID:20070627 | In vitro sensitivity study, Madang Province, Papua New Guinea (endemic)

---

### Key Associations

CQ resistance in *P. falciparum* co-occurs with resistance to other **4-aminoquinolines** via correlated IC₅₀ profiles:

| Co-occurring Resistance | Metric | Certainty | Evidence Grade |
|---|---|---|---|
| Naphthoquine resistance | IC₅₀ correlation (4-aminoquinolines) | High | Expert opinion |
| Amodiaquine resistance | IC₅₀ correlation (4-aminoquinolines) | High | Expert opinion |

---

### Synthesis

Both claims share same source, region, mechanism class. Pattern = **cross-resistance within 4-aminoquinoline class** — CQ-resistant isolates show correlated in vitro reduced sensitivity to naphthoquine and amodiaquine. Likely driven by shared resistance mechanism (pfcrt/pfmdr1 polymorphisms).

**Clinical implication:** CQ-resistant PNG isolates likely also resistant to amodiaquine and naphthoquine. Drugs from same class not reliable fallback where CQ resistance established.

---

### Caveats

- Evidence grade: expert opinion (not RCT/observational cohort) — correlation strength unquantified (no CI values)
- Region-specific: Madang Province PNG — generalizability unknown
- Status: both claims `pending_review`
- In vitro IC₅₀ correlation ≠ confirmed clinical treatment failure
