## P. falciparum Isolates — CNV Resistance Markers, Southern Angola

**Source:** PMID:39794826 | Hospital, Lubango, southern Angola (endemic)

---

### Key Findings

Both resistance-associated copy number variants (CNVs) show **low prevalence** in this population:

| Gene | Marker | Prevalence |
|------|--------|------------|
| *pfmdr1* | Increased CNV | **8.9%** |
| *pfpm2* | Increased CNV | **9.8%** |

---

### Clinical Relevance

- **pfmdr1** CNV → reduced sensitivity to mefloquine, lumefantrine (partner drug in AL)
- **pfpm2** CNV → piperaquine resistance (DHAPPQ regimens)
- Both ~9% — low but non-negligible in endemic hospital setting

---

### Evidence Quality

- Certainty: **moderate** | Grade: expert opinion
- No CIs reported — point estimates only
- Status: pending review

---

### Interpretation

Angola isolates show low amplification of both markers relative to high-burden Southeast Asian settings. Suggests AL and DHAPPQ likely retain efficacy locally, but baseline CNV surveillance warranted as drug pressure evolves.
