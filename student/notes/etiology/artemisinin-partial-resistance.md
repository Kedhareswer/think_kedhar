## Etiology of Artemisinin Partial Resistance

**Core mechanism:** *Plasmodium falciparum* Kelch 13 (K13) single point mutations → artemisinin partial resistance. Both claims high certainty, expert opinion grade.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Gene | *kelch13* (*pfk13*) |
| Mutation type | Single point mutations |
| Comparator | Wild-type K13 parasites |
| Geography | SE Asia (origin); expanding to sub-Saharan Africa — Rwanda, Uganda, Horn of Africa |
| Setting | Malaria-endemic regions |

**Sources:**
- PMID:38321292 — emergence, transmission dynamics, mechanisms in Africa
- PMID:38552654 — emergence in Africa + response framing

---

**Synthesis:**
K13 mutations are established genetic cause of artemisinin partial resistance. Mechanism likely involves K13's role in ubiquitin proteasome pathway — mutant K13 impairs PI3K-mediated phosphatidylinositol-3-phosphate signaling, reducing artemisinin activation or boosting parasite stress response. "Partial" resistance = delayed parasite clearance, not full treatment failure (ACT partners still active).

Geographic spread from SE Asia → Africa is key epidemiological concern. African context complicated by high transmission intensity and pre-existing host immunity masking clinical resistance.

**Evidence gap:** Both claims lack quantified effect size (no ORs, HRs, clearance half-lives recorded). Evidence grade is expert opinion — RCT-level mechanistic data not captured here.

**Status:** Both `pending_review`.
