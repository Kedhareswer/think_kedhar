## Etiology: Altered *P. falciparum* Susceptibility to Heme-Binding Antimalarials

**Source:** PMID:32905757 | Certainty: Moderate | Grade: Expert Opinion

---

### Mechanism Class
Heme-binding antimalarials (chloroquine, quinine, mefloquine, lumefantrine, amodiaquine) act in the parasite digestive vacuole — bind free heme, block hemozoin crystallization → toxic heme accumulation → parasite death.

Resistance alters drug access to or concentration within that vacuole.

---

### Two PfMDR1-Mediated Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Effect | Comparator |
|---|---|---|
| **Point mutations** (e.g., N86Y, Y184F, D1246Y) | Alter substrate specificity of vacuolar transporter → change drug import/export balance | Wild-type PfMDR1 |
| **Gene amplification → overexpression** | Increased pump copies → enhanced drug efflux from vacuole → reduced intra-vacuolar drug concentration | Baseline copy number |

**PfMDR1** = P-glycoprotein homolog on digestive vacuole membrane. Pumps substrates *into* vacuole (e.g., chloroquine) or *out* depending on isoform/mutation context.

---

### Key Nuances

- Mutation effects are **drug-specific and directional**: N86Y → reduced CQ susceptibility but *increased* mefloquine/lumefantrine susceptibility (trade-off).
- Overexpression → mefloquine/lumefantrine resistance (reduced vacuolar accumulation); less consistent effect on CQ.
- Both mechanisms **co-occur** in field isolates, complicating attribution.
- Evidence grade = expert opinion; direct mechanistic studies (transfection lines) support the model but effect sizes under clinical dosing remain poorly quantified.

---

### Evidence Gaps
- No IC₅₀ shifts with CIs reported in these claims
- Region-specific mutation prevalence not captured
- Interaction with *pfcrt* mutations (major CQ resistance driver) not addressed here
