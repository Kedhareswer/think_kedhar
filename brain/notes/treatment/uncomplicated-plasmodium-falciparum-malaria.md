## Treatment: Uncomplicated *Plasmodium falciparum* Malaria

### First-Line: ACTs

Artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT) = standard outpatient treatment. Artemisinin derivative + partner drug. High certainty. [PMID:38321292]

Sub-Saharan Africa: artemisinin-based regimens remain high-certainty treatment despite emerging partial resistance pressure. [PMID:38552654]

### Head-to-Head: DHA-PIP vs AL (Uganda, ages 6mo–10yr)

| Drug | PCR-corrected 28-day efficacy (95% CI) | Certainty |
|---|---|---|
| Dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DHA-PIP) | 98.9–100% | High |
| Artemether-lumefantrine (AL) | 87.2–94.4% | Moderate |

Source: 3-site Uganda RCT, outpatient, malaria-endemic. [PMID:34952573]

DHA-PIP outperforms AL on PCR-corrected efficacy at 28 days in this population.

### Resistance Context

Artemisinin partial resistance emerging in Africa — signals that efficacy advantage of DHA-PIP over AL may erode if resistance spreads. Monitor molecular markers (kelch13 mutations). Evidence grades currently logged as `expert_opinion`; claims `pending_review`.
