### Synthesis

Both claims establish artemisinin-class agents as standard of care for severe *P. falciparum* malaria in inpatient settings. Claim [3054da09] specifies **IV artesunate** as the delivery route — consistent with WHO guidance for severe disease requiring parenteral therapy. Claim [55ba697e] broadens to **combination regimens** in sub-Saharan Africa endemic context.

**Critical source context:** Both claims derive from papers on **artemisinin partial resistance (ART-R)** emergence in Africa (PMID:38321292, PMID:38552654) — not primary efficacy RCTs. Evidence grade `expert_opinion` reflects this. The treatment recommendation is sound, but the sourcing signals a **resistance caveat**: ART-R is spreading in Africa, potentially reducing parasite clearance rates and complicating outcomes even when artesunate remains first-line.

---

### Gaps / Flags

- **Dose regimen:** `mg_per_kg`, frequency, duration all `null` for both claims — standard IV artesunate dosing (2.4 mg/kg at 0, 12, 24 h, then daily) not captured
- **Comparator:** No head-to-head data encoded (e.g., vs. quinine — AQUAMAT trial context absent)
- **Population:** pregnancy status, pediatric subgroup, immune status unspecified
- **Resistance modifier:** ART-R prevalence in source regions should qualify certainty downward in affected zones; claims don't encode this
- **Evidence grade mismatch:** "high" certainty + "expert_opinion" grade is internally inconsistent — high certainty should cite RCT/systematic review
