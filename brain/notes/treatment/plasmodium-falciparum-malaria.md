## Treatment: *Plasmodium falciparum* Malaria

### Core Claim
Artemisinins — delivered as artemisinin combination therapies (ACTs) — are the global standard for treating *P. falciparum* malaria. All three claims converge on this with **high certainty**.

---

### What the Evidence Says

**First-line treatment (b864cb6a)**
ACTs are first-line across malaria-endemic regions. Key regimens:
- Artemether-lumefantrine (AL)
- Artesunate-amodiaquine (ASAQ)
- Dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DHA-PPQ)

Source models population-level optimal use — emphasis on *combination* to slow resistance.

**Mechanistic context (f55e9829, 907989a6)**
Two resistance-focused papers still affirm artemisinin efficacy as the therapeutic baseline — they frame resistance as an *emerging threat*, not current treatment failure. Key finding: Pf Coronin mutations and *kelch13* polymorphisms enable partial artemisinin resistance, but ACTs retain effectiveness where partner drugs remain active.
