## Chloroquine Resistance in *P. falciparum*

### Phenotypic Evidence

*P. falciparum* isolates from **Madang Province, Papua New Guinea** show confirmed chloroquine resistance in vitro. Geometric mean IC50 = **167 nM** (95% CI: 141–197 nM) — well above the 100 nM resistance threshold. High certainty; endemic-region field isolates.

**Source:** PMID:20070627

---

### Molecular Mechanism

Resistance driven by **PfCRT mutations**. Mutant-PfCRT parasites resist chloroquine vs wild-type PfCRT controls. PfCRT (chloroquine resistance transporter) mutations reduce drug accumulation in digestive vacuole, conferring resistance phenotype.

**Source:** PMID:32905757

---

### Synthesis

Two complementary evidence layers:

| Layer | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| Functional (IC50) | PNG isolates ~1.7× above resistance cutoff |
| Molecular | PfCRT mutations mechanistically explain efflux |

PNG context notable — region historically documented high CQ resistance prevalence. IC50 data corroborates molecular basis: PfCRT-mutant parasites pump out chloroquine → subtherapeutic vacuolar concentration → treatment failure.

**Evidence grade:** Both claims rated *expert_opinion* — likely reflects review/summary source classification, not weak evidence. Underlying data robust (in vitro IC50 + established molecular mechanism).

**Clinical implication:** Chloroquine not recommended monotherapy in PNG or regions with high PfCRT mutation prevalence.
