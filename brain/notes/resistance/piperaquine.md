## Resistance / Piperaquine

Two distinct molecular mechanisms drive *P. falciparum* piperaquine resistance.

---

### Mechanism 1 — *pfpm2* Copy Number Variation
**Subject:** pfpm2 increased CNV → **resists** piperaquine
**Region:** Southern Angola (endemic)
**Certainty:** Moderate | **Grade:** Expert opinion

Amplification of *pfpm2* (plasmepsin 2 gene) is established marker of piperaquine resistance. However, source paper (PMID:39794826) reports **low prevalence** of this CNV in southern Angola isolates — resistance mechanism present in population but not yet dominant.

---

### Mechanism 2 — PfCRT Mutations
**Subject:** *P. falciparum* with PfCRT mutations → **resists** piperaquine vs wild-type PfCRT
**Certainty:** High | **Grade:** Expert opinion

Mutations in PfCRT (chloroquine resistance transporter) confer piperaquine resistance. Mechanism established in literature (PMID:32905757); no region qualifier — considered general/global.

---

### Synthesis

| Factor | pfpm2 CNV | PfCRT mutations |
|---|---|---|
| Certainty | Moderate | High |
| Mechanism | Gene amplification | Point mutations in transporter |
| Geographic scope | Angola (low prevalence) | General |
| Clinical relevance | Monitor, not yet alarm | Active resistance marker |

Two independent resistance pathways. PfCRT mutations = higher-confidence mechanism. pfpm2 CNV = regionally variable, low in Angola currently but warrants surveillance as DHA-PPQ spreads. Both pending review — no effect sizes captured yet.
