## Topic Note: resistance/oz277

**Source:** PMID:41959371 — *Double Mutations in Plasmodium falciparum Kelch13 drive resistance to next-generation artemisinin derivatives in malaria parasites*

---

### Core Finding

K13 **A212T** secondary mutation amplifies OZ277 resistance when co-occurring with established K13 resistance alleles in *P. falciparum* (Cambodia).

### Claims

| # | Subject | vs. Comparator | Certainty | Grade |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | K13 **C580Y + A212T** | C580Y single mutant | Moderate | Expert opinion |
| 2 | K13 **R539T + A212T** | R539T single mutant | Moderate | Expert opinion |

### Synthesis

Both claims share identical structure: A212T acts as a **resistance-amplifying co-mutation** on top of existing K13 alleles (C580Y, R539T), increasing OZ277 resistance beyond what single mutations achieve. Pattern suggests A212T is not independently resistance-conferring but **synergistic** with primary K13 kelch-domain mutations.

OZ277 (arterolane) is a synthetic ozonide — not artemisinin — but shares same PfPI3K/hemoglobin-digestion activation pathway, explaining why K13 mutations confer cross-resistance.

### Gaps

- No quantitative effect sizes (IC50 fold-change, RSA survival rates) extracted
- Cambodia-specific; generalizability unknown
- Evidence grade = expert opinion only — no RCT/in-vivo data

### Status

Both claims `pending_review`. Moderate certainty reflects mechanistic plausibility without hard quantitative data.
