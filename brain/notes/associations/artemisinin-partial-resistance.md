## Artemisinin Partial Resistance — Synthesis

**Core pattern:** APR no longer SE Asia-confined. Independently emerged across eastern Africa (Rwanda, Uganda, Horn of Africa) via distinct K13 mutations — not imported lineages.

### Geographic Spread
- Previously: APR = SE Asia problem (K13 C580Y dominant)
- Now: eastern Africa *P. falciparum* transmission zones showing APR in high-endemicity settings
- African K13 variants (R561H, A675V, C469Y) arose independently → convergent evolution under artemisinin pressure [c3c53e47, 1597e761]

### Mechanistic Escalation
- Double K13 mutations now documented driving resistance to **next-generation artemisinin derivatives** — not just first-line ACTs [49b5b0e4]
- Compounds developed as resistance workarounds already under selective pressure before wide deployment

### Key Tensions
| Factor | SE Asia | Africa |
|---|---|---|
| Transmission intensity | low | high |
| Immunity buffering | minimal | partial (may mask delayed clearance) |
| K13 origin | clonal expansion | independent emergences |
| Resistance spread risk | well-characterized | unknown trajectory |

### Evidence Gaps
All 3 claims: `evidence_grade = expert_opinion`, no effect sizes. Quantitative data on APR prevalence, clearance rates, and clinical outcomes in African cohorts needed to move beyond expert consensus.

### Bottom Line
APR in Africa = independent evolutionary events, not importation. Double K13 mutations threatening next-gen drugs signals arms race accelerating. High-endemicity context may complicate detection (immune hosts clear parasites despite partial resistance). Surveillance and ACT partner-drug protection now critical.
