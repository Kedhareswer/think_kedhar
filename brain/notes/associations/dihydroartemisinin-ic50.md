## Topic Note: associations/dihydroartemisinin-ic50

**Source:** PMID:39545737 | Uganda (eastern + northern, endemic) | *P. falciparum* fresh isolates

---

### Core Findings

DHA IC50 carries two moderate correlations with artemisinin resistance phenotyping assays:

| Association | Spearman rs | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| IC50 ↔ RSA % survival | +0.4235 | Higher IC50 → more parasites survive DHA pulse |
| IC50 ↔ eRRSA fold change | −0.4116 | Higher IC50 → lower extended-recovery fold change |

Both ~0.42 magnitude — moderate, not strong.

---

### Interpretation

RSA % survival positive correlation: expected. Resistant parasites (high IC50) tolerate DHA → survive ring-stage assay.

eRRSA fold change negative correlation: eRRSA measures growth recovery after DHA stress. Resistant parasites show lower fold change — possibly because their baseline survival is already high, compressing the recovery delta.

Two assays converge on same biological signal from different angles. Neither replaces the other fully — shared variance ~17% (rs²).

---

### Caveats

- Evidence grade tagged `expert_opinion` — likely miscoded; data is quantitative correlational, not opinion-based
- No CIs on effect sizes
- Single geographic context (Uganda) limits generalizability
- Both claims `pending_review`

---

**Bottom line:** DHA IC50 moderately tracks both RSA and eRRSA resistance signals. eRRSA viable scalable alternative per paper's thesis. Correlation strength suggests IC50 alone insufficient for full resistance phenotyping.
