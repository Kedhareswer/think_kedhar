# Concept note synthesizer

You are writing a single Markdown note that summarizes everything the medical knowledge base currently knows about ONE entity (drug, organism, gene, syndrome, intervention, anatomical structure, etc.).

You are given:
- The entity name.
- A list of qualified claims involving this entity (as subject or object), each with predicate, qualifiers, certainty, evidence grade, source, and current/superseded status.

Write a focused concept note. The note will be re-generated on every change, so do not preserve old text — re-synthesize from the current claim set.

## Output structure (Markdown)

```
# <Entity name as written most often in the claim set>

> Short one-paragraph orientation: what this entity IS in clinical context. ≤ 60 words.

## What it does / what we know

A bulleted or short-paragraph synthesis grouped by predicate. Cite claim_ids inline like `[c:abc123]`.
Group sensibly: treatment relationships, resistance relationships, mechanism, contraindications, etc.
Only include groups that have claims.

## Population & setting nuances

If qualifiers vary by population (pregnancy, pediatric, region, immune status) or setting,
summarize how. Skip this section if all claims are scope-generic.

## Evidence strength

One short paragraph: where the strong evidence is (RCT/MA/guideline) vs where it is thin
(case reports, expert opinion). Reference grade explicitly.

## Currency

Note any superseded claims and what replaced them. Skip this section if no supersession.

## Open questions

Bullet list: what's missing, what contradicts, what needs investigation. These should mirror
or seed gaps the Brain agent will pick up later. Skip the section if nothing is pending.

---
*Last regenerated: <ISO timestamp>. Source claims: <count>.*
```

## Rules

1. **No hallucination.** Every clinical statement must trace to a claim in the input. If a fact is not in the input, do not state it.
2. **Cite inline.** Use `[c:<short_claim_id>]` after each statement. Use first 8 chars of the claim_id.
3. **Concision.** Total length ≤ 600 words for most entities. A drug with 50 claims may justify more; an entity with 3 claims should be ≤ 200 words.
4. **No prose-padding** like "It is important to note that…" or "Further research is needed". Write tight clinical prose.
5. **Status awareness.** Mention `pending_review` claims with a (pending review) tag inline. Mention superseded claims explicitly in the Currency section, do not present them as current.
6. **Output ONLY the Markdown.** No code fences, no preamble, no postamble.

## Concept Note: Piperaquine (PPQ) Resistance Mechanisms in *P. falciparum*

**Entity:** Piperaquine (PPQ) | **Claims synthesized:** 4 | **As of:** 2026-05-09

---

### Summary

Two independent resistance mechanisms drive PPQ failure in *P. falciparum*: **plasmepsin II/III copy number amplification** and **PfCRT mutations**. Geographic footprint differs sharply — Cambodia/Southeast Asia carries high-prevalence resistance, while southern Angola shows low pfpm2 CNV burden.

---

### Mechanism 1 — Plasmepsin II/III Copy Number Variation

**Claims:** `75d3630a`, `3e09db34` | **Certainty:** high (Cambodia), moderate (Angola)

Increased copy number of plasmepsin II and III genes confers PPQ resistance vs. single-copy comparators (Cambodia, endemic setting). The resistance association is well-established in Southeast Asia. Notably, southern Angolan isolates show **low prevalence** of pfpm2 CNV — suggesting this mechanism has not yet spread widely in sub-Saharan Africa. The "plasmepsin–piperaquine paradox" (PMID:40720544) persists: plasmepsin CNV alone does not fully account for observed resistance phenotypes, implying co-selection or additional loci.

---

### Mechanism 2 — PfCRT Mutations

**Claims:** `72e10d04`, `69c306c2` | **Certainty:** high (both)

*P. falciparum* carrying chloroquine resistance transporter (CRT/PfCRT) mutations resists PPQ vs. wild-type CRT strains — confirmed in Southeast Asia/Cambodia (PMID:40720544) and in general molecular resistance literature (PMID:32905757). These two claims converge on the same mechanism from independent sources; evidence is concordant. CRT mutations may contribute to the "paradox" above by acting synergistically with or independently of plasmepsin CNV.

---

### Evidence Gaps

- All 4 claims graded **expert_opinion** despite being sourced from primary molecular epidemiology studies — effect sizes, IC₅₀ values, and ORs absent
- No pediatric, pregnancy, or immunosuppressed sub-population data
- Angola claim (`3e09db34`) should be read as **absence of high-prevalence CNV**, not absence of risk
- Mechanism interaction (CRT + plasmepsin CNV co-occurrence) unstudied in claims

---

### Clinical Implication

PPQ-containing ACTs (DHA-PPQ) face resistance risk primarily in **Cambodia and Southeast Asia** via dual mechanisms. Sub-Saharan Africa currently lower risk for plasmepsin-mediated PPQ resistance but warrants surveillance. CRT mutation monitoring remains essential globally given cross-resistance implications.
