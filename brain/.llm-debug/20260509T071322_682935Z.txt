## Rules

1. **No hallucination.** Every clinical statement must trace to a claim in the input. If a fact is not in the input, do not state it.
2. **Cite inline.** Use `[c:<short_claim_id>]` after each statement. Use first 8 chars of the claim_id.
3. **Concision.** Total length ≤ 600 words for most entities. A drug with 50 claims may justify more; an entity with 3 claims should be ≤ 200 words.
4. **No prose-padding** like "It is important to note that…" or "Further research is needed". Write tight clinical prose.
5. **Status awareness.** Mention `pending_review` claims with a (pending review) tag inline. Mention superseded claims explicitly in the Currency section, do not present them as current.
6. **Output ONLY the Markdown.** No code fences, no preamble, no postamble.

## Concept Note: Artemisinin Partial Resistance (ART-R)

**Entity:** Artemisinin partial resistance (ART-R)
**Claims synthesized:** 7 | **Evidence grade:** Expert opinion (all) | **Certainty:** High (all)

---

### Mechanistic Basis

*P. falciparum* Kelch 13 (K13) protein mutations — specifically single point mutations in the *kelch13* gene — are the established genetic driver of ART-R (claims 471b4ef8, 28f1f4f9). Mutant parasites survive artemisinin exposure that clears wild-type strains, producing the "partial resistance" phenotype: reduced but not abolished drug susceptibility.

---

### Geographic Spread

ART-R was historically confined to Southeast Asia (Cambodia and neighboring countries; claim 5b1ba101). It has since emerged across malaria-endemic Africa — documented in eastern Africa broadly, and specifically in Rwanda, Uganda, and the Horn of Africa (claims d10e2e41, c3c53e47, 1597e761, 49b5b0e4).

---

### Independent African Emergence

African K13 variants arose **independently** of Southeast Asian lineages rather than via importation (claim 1597e761, comparator: "Southeast Asia K13 variants"). This de novo emergence implies local selective pressure is sufficient to generate resistance without gene flow from Asia — a critical epidemiological distinction with surveillance and containment implications.

---

### Diagnostic / Phenotyping Context

Extended recovery ring-stage survival assays (RSA) are proposed as scalable phenotyping tools for fresh African isolates (claim d10e2e41, PMID:39545737) — relevant for tracking spread given the difficulty of clinical endpoint studies in high-transmission settings.
