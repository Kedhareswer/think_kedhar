## Rules

1. **No hallucination.** Every statement traces to a claim in the input.
2. **Cite inline** with `[c:<short_id>]` (first 8 chars).

4. **Surface contradictions.** If two claims with similar scope conflict, name both.
5. **Status awareness.** Tag `(pending review)` for unconfirmed claims. Treat superseded claims as historical context only.
6. **Output ONLY the Markdown.**

## Resistance / Artemisinins

### Core Claims

**1. Geographic spread of partial resistance** `fc5e75f7`
*P. falciparum* resists artemisinins in **eastern Africa and southeast Asia** (malaria-endemic settings), relative to susceptible strains. Resistance now documented on two continents — no longer confined to Greater Mekong Subregion.
— PMID:38552654 | certainty: high | grade: expert opinion

**2. Molecular mechanism — PfKelch13 C580Y** `bd39741a`
**PfKelch13 C580Y mutation** drives artemisinin resistance vs wild-type PfKelch13. C580Y is dominant validated marker; disrupts Kelch propeller domain → impairs proteasome-mediated hemoglobin digestion pathway that artemisinins exploit.
— PMID:25874676 | certainty: high | grade: expert opinion

---

### Synthesis

Artemisinin partial resistance (ART-R) operates via a defined mechanism (PfKelch13 mutations, especially C580Y) and has spread beyond SEA into Africa — clinically significant because Africa carries ~90% of global malaria burden. "Partial" resistance = slower ring-stage clearance, not complete failure; partner drug resistance determines treatment outcome. Surveillance of Kelch13 allele frequencies is frontline monitoring strategy.
