## Concept Note: Autophagy as Therapeutic Target in ART-Resistant *P. falciparum* (Kelch13 C580Y)

**Entity:** *Plasmodium falciparum* — artemisinin-resistant, Kelch13 C580Y mutation
**Source:** PMID:40396048 — *"The ART of Resistance."*
**Date synthesized:** 2026-05-09

---

### Core Hypothesis

ART-resistant parasites (C580Y lineage) upregulate autophagy as compensatory survival mechanism — making them **more sensitive** to autophagy inhibition than wild-type.

---

### Evidence Summary

| Claim | Direction | Certainty | Grade | Setting |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-------|---------|
| Resistant parasites **require** autophagy for survival | Mechanism | Moderate | Expert opinion | In vitro, SE Asia |
| MRT68921 **treats** resistant parasites (↓ IC50 vs. WT) | Therapeutic | Moderate | Expert opinion | In vitro, SE Asia |

Comparator: isogenic wild-type PfKelch13 — controls for genetic background.

---

### Mechanistic Logic

```
ART pressure
    → C580Y mutation → reduced ART activation sensitivity
    → parasite survives ring stage
    → elevated proteotoxic / oxidative stress
    → compensatory autophagy upregulation
        → vulnerability to autophagy inhibitor (MRT68921)
```

Resistance mechanism creates collateral dependency. Classic synthetic lethality logic.

---

### Therapeutic Implication

MRT68921 exploits resistance-induced autophagy upregulation. IC50 **lower** in resistant vs. WT — inverse of typical resistance profile. Potential combination: ART + MRT68921 to prevent or reverse resistance.

---

### Gaps & Caveats

- No IC50 values reported — magnitude of differential unknown
- Evidence grade: expert opinion, not primary experimental data
- In vitro only — no in vivo PK/PD, no animal model
- MRT68921 selectivity (mammalian vs. parasite ULK1/2) not addressed
- C580Y single mutation — other resistance haplotypes not characterized
- Region-specific claim (SE Asia) — generalizability unconfirmed

---
