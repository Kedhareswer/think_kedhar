## Concept Note: Pfcoronin Mutations in Artemisinin Resistance

**Source:** PMID:40964400 | **Claims:** 7 | **Date:** 2026-05-09

---

### Mechanism Chain

Pfcoronin mutations disrupt actin-binding (PfCoronin–PfActin interaction impaired [e731f6b4]) → PfCoronin loses ring-stage localization [5d1c7872] → endocytosis impaired in ring stage [6ab65186] → reduced hemoglobin acquisition [441ef574] → less heme substrate for artemisinin activation → artemisinin resistance [e346c10f] → treatment failure in Africa [c2d0644a].

---

### Convergence with Pfkelch13

Pfcoronin mutations co-occur with Pfkelch13 mutations; both converge on heme limitation as resistance mechanism [bc627d75]. Certainty **low** — convergence claim is expert inference, not direct demonstration.

### Key Gaps

- No quantitative effect sizes for any claim
- No population-level epidemiological data linking mutation prevalence to clinical failure rates
- Convergence with Pfkelch13 mechanism (heme limitation) is low-certainty — needs co-culture / genetic interaction studies
- Ring-stage localization loss: phenotype described, structural mechanism not characterized

---

### Synthesis

Pfcoronin mutations confer artemisinin resistance via actin-pathway disruption of hemoglobin endocytosis — starving the parasite ring stage of heme, which artemisinin requires for activation. Pathway is mechanistically coherent but evidence base is expert-opinion grade with no quantified effect sizes. Clinical relevance in Africa is asserted (moderate certainty) but lacks epidemiological support. Convergence with Pfkelch13 is the most speculative claim and warrants targeted investigation.
