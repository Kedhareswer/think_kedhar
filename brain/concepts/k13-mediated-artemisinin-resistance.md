## Concept Note: K13-Mediated Artemisinin Resistance

**Entity:** K13-mediated artemisinin resistance (*Plasmodium falciparum*)

---

### Summary

K13-mediated artemisinin resistance operates via disruption of hemoglobin endocytosis and co-evolves with mutations in at least two accessory genes across endemic field populations.

---

### Mechanism

Resistance requires disruption of hemoglobin endocytosis (moderate certainty; expert opinion — PMID:32905757). K13 mutations reduce parasite uptake of hemoglobin during ring stage, limiting intracellular heme available to activate artemisinin. Less drug activation = parasite survival despite exposure.

---

### Co-occurring Genomic Variants

Cross-national genomic surveillance (MalariaGEN Network, 15 countries, field isolates) identified two genes with mutations co-existing alongside K13 variants at higher SNP counts than in artemisinin-sensitive isolates (PMID:35464842; moderate certainty; treated as meta-analysis):

| Gene | Role | Evidence |
|------|------|----------|
| Putative HECT domain-containing protein | Ubiquitin ligase — likely intersects protein degradation/trafficking | Co-occurrence signal |
| α-β hydrolase | Metabolic/esterase function — mechanism unclear | Co-occurrence signal |

Both are candidate accessory resistance loci; causal roles not established.

---

### Evidence Gaps

- Effect sizes (OR/HR) absent for co-occurrence claims
- No mechanistic link shown for HECT or α-β hydrolase genes
- Hemoglobin endocytosis claim rests on expert opinion, not experimental trial data
- No population or demographic breakdown in mechanistic claim
