## Concept Note: Reduced Hemoglobin Acquisition

**Entity:** Reduced hemoglobin acquisition
**Variant in focus:** Reduced hemoglobin acquisition in *Plasmodium falciparum*
**Source:** PMID:40964400 (Pfcoronin & artemisinin resistance)

---

### Summary

Two linked claims from same source establish a causal chain:

```
Pfcoronin mutations
    → reduced hemoglobin acquisition (in P. falciparum)
        → limited heme availability for artemisinin activation
            → artemisinin resistance
```

**Claim 1 — Upstream cause** (`441ef574`)
- **Subject:** Pfcoronin mutations
- **Effect:** Reduced hemoglobin acquisition vs. wild-type Pfcoronin
- **Certainty:** Moderate | **Grade:** Expert opinion

**Claim 2 — Downstream consequence** (`173454bb`)
- **Subject:** Reduced hemoglobin acquisition
- **Effect:** Limited heme availability for artemisinin activation
- **Drug context:** Artemisinin
- **Certainty:** Moderate | **Grade:** Expert opinion

---

### Mechanism logic

Artemisinin requires heme (from hemoglobin digestion) for activation into toxic radical species. Pfcoronin mutations impair hemoglobin uptake/processing → less substrate → less drug activation → parasite survives. Both claims moderate certainty, expert-opinion grade — mechanism is mechanistically plausible but lacks quantified effect sizes.
