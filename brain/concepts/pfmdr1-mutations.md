## Concept Note: pfmdr1 mutations

**Entity:** PfMDR1 (P. falciparum multidrug resistance protein 1)
**Key variants:** N86Y, Y184F, D1246Y

---

### Summary

PfMDR1 mutations associate with two distinct but related resistance phenotypes: altered parasite susceptibility to heme-binding antimalarials (mechanistic) and clinical ACT treatment failure (epidemiological).

---

### Claim Synthesis

| # | Predicate | Object | Evidence | Certainty |
|---|-----------|--------|----------|-----------|
| [85ff13b2](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33556786/) | co-occurs with | ACT (AL) treatment failure | Meta-analysis | Moderate |
| [790f5e7b](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32905757/) | causes | altered susceptibility to heme-binding antimalarials | Expert opinion | Moderate |

---

### Synthesis

Mechanistically (790f5e7b), PfMDR1 mutations shift parasite drug susceptibility — likely via altered drug transport/sequestration relative to wild-type. This underpins the clinical signal: specific mutations (86Y, 184F, 1246Y) co-occur with artemether-lumefantrine failure across sub-Saharan Africa and India in meta-analytic data (85ff13b2).

**Key gap:** No effect sizes captured in either claim. Mechanistic claim is expert opinion only — causal arrow from PfMDR1 variant → susceptibility shift lacks quantified in-vivo support here.

**Convergence:** Both claims moderate certainty. Meta-analysis (85ff13b2) > expert opinion (790f5e7b) in evidential weight, but they operate at different levels (clinical vs. molecular).
