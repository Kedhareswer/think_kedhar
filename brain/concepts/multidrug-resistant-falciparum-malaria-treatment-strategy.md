## Concept Note: Multidrug-Resistant *Falciparum* Malaria — Treatment Strategy

**Source:** PMID:34267045 | *Artemisinin and multidrug-resistant Plasmodium falciparum — a threat for malaria control and elimination.*

---

### Core Position

Against multidrug-resistant (MDR) *P. falciparum*, two complementary strategic pivots are recommended over conventional single-agent ACT:

| # | Recommendation | Comparator | Certainty | Grade |
|---|---------------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | **Multiple first-line therapies** deployed in rotation/combination | Single first-line ACT | Moderate | Expert opinion |
| 2 | **Triple drug combination (TDC) therapy** | Dual ACT therapy | Moderate | Expert opinion |

---

### Synthesis

Both claims share the same epistemic source and signal a **layered resistance-mitigation doctrine**:

- **Claim 1** operates at the *policy/system level* — diversifying the first-line treatment portfolio to reduce selective pressure from any single regimen across a population.
- **Claim 2** operates at the *patient/regimen level* — adding a third active drug to overwhelm parasites with partial resistance to artemisinin or its partner drug.

Together they form a coherent escalation logic:

> Artemisinin partial resistance → ACT partner drug resistance → neither monotherapy nor dual ACT sufficient → deploy TDC *and* rotate multiple first-line options across endemic settings.
