## Concept Note: *Plasmodium falciparum* Malaria — Treatment & Resistance

**Entity:** Plasmodium falciparum malaria
**Claims synthesized:** 4 | **Date:** 2026-05-09

---

### Core Treatment Landscape

Artemisinins and their combination therapies (ACTs) are backbone of *P. falciparum* treatment globally. Three claims (b864cb6a, f55e9829, 907989a6) converge on same predicate — `treats` — with high certainty, expert-opinion grade. Specific ACT regimens named: artemether-lumefantrine, artesunate-amodiaquine, dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine. ACTs positioned as **first-line** in malaria-endemic settings.

### Resistance Threat

Claim 49b5b0e4 breaks from treatment narrative: artemisinin **partial resistance** documented co-occurring with *P. falciparum* in **SE Asia and Africa**. Source (PMID:41959371, 2025) identifies double mutations in *Kelch13* driving resistance to next-generation artemisinin derivatives — expanding beyond legacy mutations. PMID:40964400 (Coronin-mediated resistance) and PMID:25874676 (molecular mechanism of resistance) reinforce that resistance is mechanistically multifactorial.

### Synthesis

| Dimension | Summary |
|-----------|---------|
| **Treatment** | ACTs = global standard, high certainty |
| **Resistance** | Partial, spreading — SE Asia origin, now Africa |
| **Mechanism** | Kelch13 double mutants + Coronin pathway |
| **Risk** | Next-gen artemisinins also affected (2025 data) |

### Evidence Gaps

All 4 claims lack dose/regimen quantification (mg/kg, duration null). No RCT-grade evidence cited — all `expert_opinion`. Effect sizes absent across all claims. Population subgroups (pregnancy, pediatric, immunity) unspecified.

### Implication

Resistance now threatens both legacy and next-generation artemisinins. Pipeline pressure rising. ACT monotherapy components losing efficacy in key endemic regions. Surveillance of Kelch13 + Coronin mutations critical for treatment protocol updates.
