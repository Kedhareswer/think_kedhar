## Concept Note: Artemisinin-Resistant *Plasmodium falciparum*

**Entity:** Artemisinin-resistant *Plasmodium falciparum*
**Synthesized:** 2026-05-09 | **Claims:** 4 | **Sources:** 3 PMIDs

---

### Core Resistance Profile

Artemisinin-resistant *P. falciparum* resists both artemisinin monotherapy components (e.g., dihydroartemisinin) and full artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs). Resistance operates at multiple levels:

- **Monotherapy failure** — In vitro evidence confirms DHA monotherapy is compromised against resistant strains (PMID:42059554; expert opinion, high certainty).
- **ACT failure** — Cohort-level evidence from a multicenter trial (Kenya, Peru, Thailand) documents both direct ACT resistance and reduced clinical effectiveness globally (PMID:40614930; cohort, high certainty).
- **Regional entrenchment** — ACT resistance is well-established in the Greater Mekong Subregion, with multidrug co-resistance compounding the threat (PMID:34267045; expert opinion, high certainty).

All 4 claims carry **high certainty**. Two independent evidence streams converge:

Claims `70388c43` and `b8491db4` share source PMID:40614930 — resistance and downstream effectiveness loss are two faces of the same clinical finding.

### Key Gaps

- No quantified effect sizes (IC₅₀, treatment failure rates, ORs) across any claim.
- Population qualifiers largely absent — no age, pregnancy, or immune-status stratification.
- Mechanism of resistance (kelch13 mutations, dormancy) not captured in current claim set.

---

### Implications

Partial artemisinin resistance is no longer a regional anomaly — multicenter cohort data place it globally. ACT backbone drugs (lumefantrine, piperaquine) now carry disproportionate burden. Pipeline alternatives (paclitaxel-artemisinin synergy, per PMID:42059554) show early promise but remain pre-clinical.
