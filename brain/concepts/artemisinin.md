## Concept Note: Artemisinin Resistance in *Plasmodium falciparum*

**Entity:** Artemisinin | **Claims analyzed:** 15 | **Date:** 2026-05-09

---

### Core Assertion

*P. falciparum* has evolved partial resistance to artemisinin (ART), documented across Southeast Asia, sub-Saharan Africa, and India. Resistance is **not monolithic** — it is driven by distinct genetic loci and at least two independent cellular survival pathways.

---

### Genetic Drivers

**Kelch13 (PfK13) — primary, high-certainty locus**

| Mutation | Region | Certainty | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| C580Y | Greater Mekong / SE Asia | High | Expert opinion |
| R539T, Y493H | SE Asia | High | Expert opinion |
| R561H | NW Tanzania | High | Expert opinion |
| F476I, R539T, Y561H | Sub-Saharan Africa + India | Moderate | Meta-analysis |

K13 mutations validated by WHO; C580Y is dominant in GMS. Spread to Africa (Tanzania R561H) signals geographic expansion. Meta-analysis across 15 countries (MalariaGEN) confirms co-occurring co-resistance mutations amplify K13 phenotype.

**Pfcoronin — secondary, low–moderate certainty locus**

Mutations G50E, R100K, E107V found in African endemic isolates. Mechanistic basis (actin-binding, cytoskeletal remodeling) now partially characterized. Evidence grade: expert opinion; certainty low–moderate. Not yet WHO-validated.

---

### Cellular & Molecular Mechanisms

Three non-exclusive mechanisms, all in vitro:

1. **Proteostasis / autophagy upregulation** *(moderate certainty)*
   K13 C580Y drives autophagy to clear ART-damaged proteins → parasites survive drug pulse. Directly tied to ring-stage tolerance window.

2. **ER stress induction by ART** *(moderate certainty)*
   ART causes ER stress in *P. falciparum*; resistant parasites tolerate or resolve this stress more effectively. Mechanism under investigation.

3. **Fatty acid metabolism remodeling** *(moderate certainty)*
   K13 C580Y mutants show ART-insensitive fatty acid-associated metabolic activity vs. WT. Multi-omics evidence; stage-specific (ring stage).

All three converge: **ring-stage quiescence + proteostatic buffering = partial ART tolerance**.

---

### Phenotypic Assays

- **Ring-stage Survival Assay (RSA):** standard in vitro correlate; extended recovery variant (xRSA) shows superior correlation with patient clearance half-life and higher throughput (PMID:32005233).
- **Flow cytometry RSA:** enables higher-throughput discrimination of resistant vs. sensitive isolates (PMID:24867976).
- **Clinical marker:** prolonged parasite clearance half-life in patients — the field gold standard.

---

### Key Gaps

- **Pfcoronin:** no WHO validation, sparse quantitative effect sizes
- **Effect sizes absent** across all 15 claims — no RSA survival rates, no clearance half-life values quantified
- **Autophagy / ER stress / fatty acid mechanisms** lack in vivo confirmation
- **Causal link** between pfcoronin mutations and clinical outcomes unestablished
- All mechanistic claims (autophagy, ER stress, FA metabolism) are in vitro; translation to field outcomes inferred

---

### Synthesis Statement

Artemisinin partial resistance in *P. falciparum* is **genetically heterogeneous** (K13 primary; pfcoronin secondary) and **mechanistically multi-layered** (proteostasis, ER stress tolerance, metabolic rewiring). K13 C580Y/Southeast Asian lineage is best-characterized; African emergence via R561H and pfcoronin variants is an escalating concern. Phenotypic surveillance via RSA and molecular surveillance of both K13 and pfcoronin are warranted globally. The absence of quantitative effect sizes across claims is a critical evidence gap for risk modeling.
