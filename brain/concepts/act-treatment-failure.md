## Concept Note: ACT Treatment Failure

**Entity:** ACT treatment failure
**Claims synthesized:** 5 | **Sources:** 2 (PMID:33556786, PMID:37269964)
**Date:** 2026-05-09

---

### Summary

ACT treatment failure in *P. falciparum* malaria is driven by mutations across multiple resistance genes, with partner-drug resistance playing a critical amplifying role alongside artemisinin resistance itself.

---

### Key Associations

#### Partner-drug resistance markers (AL regimen)
**Pfmdr1** mutations **86Y, 184F, 1246Y** co-occur with AL treatment failure in sub-Saharan Africa and India. *(Moderate certainty; meta-analysis — PMID:33556786)*

#### Partner-drug resistance markers (AS+SP regimen)
Two gene loci implicated in SP resistance co-occur with AS+SP failure:

- **Pfdhps 437G** — antifolate resistance marker *(Moderate; meta-analysis)*
- **Pfdhfr triple mutant (51I/59R/108N)** — antifolate resistance triple mutant *(Moderate; meta-analysis)*

#### Chloroquine resistance marker (pan-ACT)
**Pfcrt 76T** co-occurs with ACT failure broadly, not regimen-specific. *(Moderate; meta-analysis — PMID:33556786)*

#### Mechanistic driver
**Artemisinin resistance + partner drug resistance co-occurring** causally produces ACT failure. Dual resistance eliminates both components of the combination. *(High certainty; expert opinion — PMID:37269964)*

---

### Synthesis

Evidence from two independent sources converges: ACT failure is **polygenic and regimen-specific**. Mutations in *Pfmdr1* threaten AL efficacy; *Pfdhfr/Pfdhps* mutations threaten AS+SP; *Pfcrt* 76T cuts across ACTs generally. The causal mechanism — artemisinin resistance compounded by partner-drug resistance — is rated high certainty, though that claim rests on expert opinion rather than trial data.

**Evidence gap:** No effect sizes (ORs, HRs, prevalence deltas) captured across any claim. Causal directionality unquantified. All claims `pending_review`.

**Geographic scope:** Sub-Saharan Africa + India. Findings may not generalize to Southeast Asia, where *kelch13* mutation landscape differs substantially.
