## Concept Note — Kelch13 (K13) Mutations

**Entity:** *Plasmodium falciparum* kelch13 (K13) gene mutations
**As of:** 2026-05-09

---

### Core Claims

| # | Relationship | Context | Certainty | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Co-occurs with artemisinin partial resistance | SE Asia, malaria-endemic | High | Expert opinion [PMID:32005233] |
| 2 | Causes prolonged parasite clearance half-life (mean **4.1 h**) vs wild-type | Thailand, clinical trial, artesunate 3-dose | Moderate | Cohort [PMID:40614930] |
| 3 | Confers artemisinin resistance | 15 countries, MalariaGEN field data | High | Meta-analysis [PMID:35464842] |

---

### Synthesis

K13 mutations = primary molecular marker of *P. falciparum* artemisinin partial resistance. Three converging evidence streams:

- **Epidemiological:** SE Asia co-occurrence well-established (expert-opinion level; mechanistic causal direction assumed, not proven by this source alone).
- **Clinical phenotype:** Cohort data from Thailand quantifies effect — 4.1 h mean clearance half-life in K13-mutant vs wild-type infections under standard artesunate. Moderate certainty; no CI reported.
- **Field genomics:** Meta-analysis across 15-country MalariaGEN network confirms K13-resistance association at scale; also flags co-occurring mutations and expression trends that may modulate resistance.

**Key gap:** Claim 1 evidence grade = expert opinion despite high certainty label. Claim 2 lacks CI data. No effect size reported for Claim 3.

**Direction of evidence:** All three converge — K13 mutations → reduced artemisinin efficacy → slower parasite clearance. Causal chain well-supported at population and clinical levels.
