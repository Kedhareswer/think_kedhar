## Concept Note: Chloroquine Resistance in *Plasmodium falciparum*

**Source:** PMID:20070627 — *In vitro sensitivity of P. falciparum to conventional and novel antimalarial drugs in Papua New Guinea*
**Region:** Madang Province / Papua New Guinea (endemic setting)
**Evidence grade:** Expert opinion | **Certainty:** High (all claims)

---

### Mechanism

**pfcrt** resistance allele drives CQ resistance in PNG *P. falciparum* isolates. Allele prevalence data confirm causal link; quantitative effect size not reported.

### Cross-Resistance Pattern

CQ resistance co-occurs with resistance to two other **4-aminoquinolines**:

| Co-resistant drug | Metric | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Amodiaquine | IC50 correlation | Correlated in vitro |
| Naphthoquine | IC50 correlation | Correlated in vitro |

All three drugs share the 4-aminoquinoline scaffold — shared pfcrt-mediated efflux mechanism likely explains co-resistance.

### Implications

- Switching from CQ → amodiaquine in PNG likely selects on pre-existing cross-resistant parasites; efficacy gains may be limited.
- Naphthoquine (used in fixed-dose ACT combinations) faces same liability.
- Novel drugs outside 4-aminoquinoline class warranted for PNG.

### Gaps

- No IC50 values or CIs reported — magnitude of correlations unknown.
- Evidence graded *expert opinion* despite in vitro data; formal resistance thresholds and clinical correlation absent.
- pfcrt allele type (e.g., K76T vs. PNG-specific haplotypes) not specified.
