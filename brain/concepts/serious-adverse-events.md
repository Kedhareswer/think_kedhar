## Concept Note: Serious Adverse Events — DHA-PPQ vs AL

**Entity:** Serious adverse events
**Source:** PMID:34952573 | Uganda RCT

---

### Summary

Both **dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DHA-PPQ)** and **artemether-lumefantrine (AL)** show **zero serious adverse events** (SAE rate = 0.0) in head-to-head comparison.

---

### Claims

| Drug | Comparator | SAE Rate | Population | Setting |
|------|-----------|----------|------------|---------|
| DHA-PPQ | AL | 0.0 | 6 mo–10 yr, Uganda | Outpatient, malaria-endemic |
| AL | DHA-PPQ | 0.0 | 6 mo–10 yr, Uganda | Outpatient, malaria-endemic |

---

### Evidence Quality

- **Certainty:** Moderate
- **Grade:** Expert opinion
- **Status:** Pending review
- **CI:** Not reported — limits precision interpretation

---

### Key Points

1. No SAEs in either arm → both drugs appear safe in pediatric outpatient context
2. SAE rate 0.0 with no CI means can't rule out rare events — sample size matters
3. Evidence grade `expert_opinion` is lower than expected for a comparative trial — review grading
4. Uganda-specific; generalizability to other endemic regions needs separate claims

---

### Gaps / Flags

- Missing dose/kg and regimen details on both claims
- `valid_from`/`valid_until` null — temporal validity unanchored
- `expert_opinion` grade inconsistent with apparent RCT design — verify grading logic
- Two symmetric claims from same PMID — confirm deduplication policy for bidirectional comparators
