## Concept Note — OZ277

**Drug class:** Synthetic ozonide (next-generation artemisinin derivative)

---

### Resistance Signals — Double K13 Mutations

Source: [PMID:41959371](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41959371/) · *Double Mutations in Plasmodium falciparum Kelch13 drive resistance to next-generation artemisinin derivatives*

| Mutation pair | Comparator | Region | Certainty |
|---|---|---|---|
| K13 **C580Y + A212T** | C580Y single mutant | Cambodia | Moderate |
| K13 **R539T + A212T** | R539T single mutant | Cambodia | Moderate |

**Pattern:** A212T acts as additive/synergistic second hit on top of established K13 resistance alleles (C580Y, R539T), amplifying OZ277 resistance beyond single-mutant baseline.

---

### Key Implications

- OZ277 not immune to K13-based resistance evolution
- A212T alone insufficient — only confers OZ277 resistance *in cis* with primary K13 allele
- Cambodia surveillance context: C580Y dominant SEA allele; double mutants represent emergent threat
- Evidence grade **expert_opinion** (pending higher-grade in vivo/clinical confirmation); both claims `pending_review`

---

### Gaps

- No quantitative IC₅₀ / RSA effect sizes captured
- No clinical outcome data (dose-response, treatment failure rates)
- No data outside Cambodia; generalizability unknown
- Mechanism of A212T contribution to ozonide resistance uncharacterized

---

**Bottom line:** K13 double mutants (primary allele + A212T) reduce OZ277 efficacy vs. single mutants in Cambodian *P. falciparum*. Moderate certainty, expert-opinion grade. Warrants molecular surveillance for A212T co-occurrence with C580Y/R539T in OZ277 deployment regions.
