## Concept Note: pfmdr1 Increased Copy Number Variation

**Source:** PMID:39794826 — *Low prevalence of copy number variation in pfmdr1 and pfpm2 in Plasmodium falciparum isolates from southern Angola.*

---

### What it is
*pfmdr1* (P. falciparum multidrug resistance gene 1) increased copy number variation (CNV) = amplification of gene copies above diploid baseline in *P. falciparum* genome.

---

### Epidemiology (Claim 18cd150b)
- **Prevalence:** 8.9% of isolates, Lubango, southern Angola (hospital, endemic)
- **Certainty:** moderate | **Grade:** expert opinion | **Status:** pending review
- Low prevalence = CNV not dominant resistance driver in this population currently

---

### Drug Resistance Links

| Claim | Drug | Predicate | Certainty | Evidence Grade |
|-------|------|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| 6c09f243 | Lumefantrine | resists | **low** | expert opinion |
| ba628693 | Mefloquine | resists | **moderate** | expert opinion |

- Lumefantrine resistance link: effect size not quantified, signal weak
- Mefloquine resistance link: better established mechanistically; CNV → increased drug efflux via Pgh1 protein

---

### Clinical Relevance
- Lumefantrine is partner drug in AL (artemether-lumefantrine), first-line in Angola
- 8.9% CNV prevalence = low but non-zero threat to AL efficacy
- Mefloquine not first-line in Angola; resistance marker still relevant for regional spread tracking

---

### Evidence Gaps
- No quantified effect size for lumefantrine susceptibility reduction
- All claims expert opinion grade — no RCT or controlled in vivo data
- Single study, single site (Lubango); generalizability to northern/central Angola unknown
- No temporal trend data — stable vs. emerging CNV expansion unclear

---

**Bottom line:** pfmdr1 CNV rare in southern Angola (1-in-11 isolates). Moderate confidence it drives mefloquine resistance; weak confidence for lumefantrine. AL first-line remains viable but warrants continued molecular surveillance.
