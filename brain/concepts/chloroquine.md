## Chloroquine — Concept Note

**Entity:** Chloroquine
**Claims synthesized:** 2 | **Status:** Pending review | **As of:** 2026-05-09

---

### Core Concept

Chloroquine (antimalarial drug) faces clinically significant resistance in *Plasmodium falciparum*, established via both **in vitro pharmacological** and **molecular mechanistic** evidence.

---

### Claim Summary

| # | Subject | Finding | Region | Source |
|---|---------|---------|--------|--------|
| 1 | *P. falciparum* (field isolates) | Geometric mean IC₅₀ = **167 nM** (95% CI: 141–197 nM) — exceeds 100 nM resistance threshold | Madang Province, Papua New Guinea | PMID:20070627 |
| 2 | *P. falciparum* with **PfCRT mutations** | Resistant vs. wild-type PfCRT parasites | Not specified | PMID:32905757 |

---

### Synthesis

Two independent lines of evidence converge:

- **Phenotypic:** Field isolates from endemic PNG show IC₅₀ 67% above resistance threshold — active, high-level resistance in circulating parasites.
- **Mechanistic:** PfCRT (chloroquine resistance transporter) mutations drive resistance. Mutation = resistance; wild-type = susceptible. Mechanistic basis explains phenotypic observations.

**Together:** Chloroquine resistance in *P. falciparum* is both documented in the field (PNG) and mechanistically grounded in PfCRT mutations. Neither claim specifies dose regimen or quantifies effect size for the PfCRT claim.

---

### Certainty & Gaps

- Both claims: **high certainty**, graded as expert opinion
- Gaps: No dose-response data for PfCRT claim; geographic scope of claim 2 unknown; no clinical outcome data in either source

---

### Implication

Chloroquine monotherapy contraindicated for *P. falciparum* in PNG and likely wherever PfCRT mutant strains circulate. PfCRT mutation status = key biomarker for resistance prediction.
