## Concept Note: *Plasmodium falciparum* kelch13 Mutation Selection — Uganda vs Southeast Asia

**Source:** PMID:40112841 | Cohort/modelling study | MalariaGEN Pf7 + Ugandan clinical surveillance

---

### Background

Artemisinin partial resistance (ART-R) in *P. falciparum* driven by *kelch13* (K13) mutations. SE Asia — especially Cambodia–Thailand–Vietnam corridor — established reservoir. Africa showing independent emergence. This study models annual selection coefficients (s) to compare selection pressure across regions.

---

### Key Findings by Region

#### Uganda — Four mutations under surveillance

| Mutation | s (annual) | 95% CI | Certainty |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ala675Val** | 0.237 | 0.087–0.403 | High ✓ |
| **Cys469Tyr** | 0.383 | 0.207–0.591 | High ✓ |
| Cys469Phe | 0.324 | −0.629–1.150 | Low |
| Pro441Leu | 0.494 | −0.462–1.410 | Low |

**Ala675Val** and **Cys469Tyr** — both CIs exclude zero → confirmed positive selection in Uganda. **Cys469Phe** and **Pro441Leu** — CIs cross zero → selection signal present but imprecise; data insufficient to confirm.

#### Southeast Asia — Dominant mutation

| Mutation | s (annual) | 95% CI | Certainty |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cys580Tyr** | 0.627 | −0.088–1.312 | Moderate |

Highest point estimate of all five mutations. CI barely crosses zero → strong signal but wide uncertainty, likely reflects dataset heterogeneity across Cambodia/Thailand/Vietnam. Cys580Tyr is WHO-validated ART-R marker; dominates SE Asia clonal lineages.

---

### Cross-Regional Synthesis

**Divergent mutation landscapes.** Uganda mutations (Ala675Val, Cys469Tyr) differ from SE Asia's Cys580Tyr → independent origins, not importation. Africa not on same evolutionary trajectory as SE Asia.

**Selection magnitude.** Cys580Tyr point estimate (0.627) > Uganda high-certainty mutations (0.237–0.383). SE Asia selection pressure may be higher — consistent with greater ART drug exposure and lower transmission intensity (immunity pressure less masking fitness).

**Certainty contrast.** Uganda's Ala675Val and Cys469Tyr achieve *high* certainty despite lower s values → tighter CIs from longer/denser longitudinal Ugandan surveillance. SE Asia moderate certainty despite large s → noisier signal across multi-country MalariaGEN pooled data.

---

### Implications

1. **Surveillance priority:** Ala675Val + Cys469Tyr confirmed Ugandan markers → include in national resistance monitoring panels.
2. **Cys469Phe / Pro441Leu:** Underpowered. More temporal sampling needed before policy action.
3. **Cys580Tyr in Africa:** Not detected here as dominant; ongoing surveillance required given traveler/import risk.
4. **Treatment policy:** Uganda high-transmission context still suppresses clinical ART-R expression (partial immunity buffer), but selection ongoing → window before clinical failure narrows.

---

| Claim | Certainty | Limiting factor |
|---|---|---|

| Cys469Phe co-occurs with Uganda ART-R selection | Low | Wide CI, small n |

---

**Bottom line:** Uganda has independent ART-R emergence via Ala675Val and Cys469Tyr with confirmed selection. SE Asia's Cys580Tyr shows stronger (but noisier) selection. Different mutations, different trajectories — convergent threat.
