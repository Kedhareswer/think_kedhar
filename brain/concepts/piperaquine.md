## Concept Note: Piperaquine (PPQ) Resistance Mechanisms in *P. falciparum*

**Entity:** Piperaquine (PPQ) | **Claims synthesized:** 4 | **As of:** 2026-05-09

---

### Summary

Two independent resistance mechanisms drive PPQ failure in *P. falciparum*: **plasmepsin II/III copy number amplification** and **PfCRT mutations**. Geographic footprint differs sharply — Cambodia/Southeast Asia carries high-prevalence resistance, while southern Angola shows low pfpm2 CNV burden.

---

### Mechanism 1 — Plasmepsin II/III Copy Number Variation

**Claims:** `75d3630a`, `3e09db34` | **Certainty:** high (Cambodia), moderate (Angola)

Increased copy number of plasmepsin II and III genes confers PPQ resistance vs. single-copy comparators (Cambodia, endemic setting). The resistance association is well-established in Southeast Asia. Notably, southern Angolan isolates show **low prevalence** of pfpm2 CNV — suggesting this mechanism has not yet spread widely in sub-Saharan Africa. The "plasmepsin–piperaquine paradox" (PMID:40720544) persists: plasmepsin CNV alone does not fully account for observed resistance phenotypes, implying co-selection or additional loci.

---

### Mechanism 2 — PfCRT Mutations

**Claims:** `72e10d04`, `69c306c2` | **Certainty:** high (both)

*P. falciparum* carrying chloroquine resistance transporter (CRT/PfCRT) mutations resists PPQ vs. wild-type CRT strains — confirmed in Southeast Asia/Cambodia (PMID:40720544) and in general molecular resistance literature (PMID:32905757). These two claims converge on the same mechanism from independent sources; evidence is concordant. CRT mutations may contribute to the "paradox" above by acting synergistically with or independently of plasmepsin CNV.

---

### Evidence Gaps

- All 4 claims graded **expert_opinion** despite being sourced from primary molecular epidemiology studies — effect sizes, IC₅₀ values, and ORs absent
- No pediatric, pregnancy, or immunosuppressed sub-population data
- Angola claim (`3e09db34`) should be read as **absence of high-prevalence CNV**, not absence of risk
- Mechanism interaction (CRT + plasmepsin CNV co-occurrence) unstudied in claims

---

### Clinical Implication

PPQ-containing ACTs (DHA-PPQ) face resistance risk primarily in **Cambodia and Southeast Asia** via dual mechanisms. Sub-Saharan Africa currently lower risk for plasmepsin-mediated PPQ resistance but warrants surveillance. CRT mutation monitoring remains essential globally given cross-resistance implications.
