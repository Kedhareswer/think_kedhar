## Concept Note: Artemether-Lumefantrine

**Source:** PMID:34952573 | Uganda, outpatient, children 6 mo–10 yr | Comparator: dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DHP)

---

### Efficacy

AL treats uncomplicated *P. falciparum* malaria with **PCR-corrected 28-day efficacy 87.2–94.4%** (moderate certainty). Meets WHO ≥90% threshold at aggregate level, but **site-level data for Busia and Arua show CI upper bound touching 90%** — borderline performance at those sentinel sites (moderate certainty).

Uncorrected 28-day efficacy drops sharply: **cumulative recurrence range 28.8–58.8%** (efficacy CI 41.2–71.2%, high certainty). Much of this reflects reinfection in high-transmission Uganda rather than true treatment failure, but the raw burden of re-presentation is high.

### Resistance Signal

*P. falciparum* resistance to AL flagged at **Busia and Arua** — PCR-corrected efficacy CI upper bound ≤90% (WHO action threshold). Molecular marker prevalence data in the source likely corroborates this. Warrants surveillance escalation at these sites.

### Safety

**Zero serious adverse events** recorded in the AL arm (rate = 0.0, moderate certainty). Both AL and DHP well-tolerated in this pediatric outpatient population.

### Synthesis

AL remains efficacious for uncomplicated *P. falciparum* in Uganda overall, but:
- **Geographically uneven** — two of three sentinel sites near or below WHO efficacy threshold
- **High reinfection burden** in 28-day window reflects endemic pressure, not drug failure per se, but stresses health system
- **Safety profile clean** — not a limiting factor

**Clinical implication:** AL continues as viable first-line option, but resistance signals at Busia/Arua justify enhanced molecular surveillance and consideration of DHP rotation if PCR-corrected efficacy falls below 90% in repeat surveys.
