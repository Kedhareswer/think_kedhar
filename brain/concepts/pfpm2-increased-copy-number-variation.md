## Concept Note: pfpm2 Increased Copy Number Variation

**Entity:** *pfpm2* increased copy number variation (CNV)
**Source:** PMID:39794826 | Southern Angola

---

### What It Is

*pfpm2* (plasmepsin 2) gene — when present in increased copy number in *Plasmodium falciparum* — confers resistance to **piperaquine**, the partner drug in dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DHA-PPQ) combination therapy.

---

### Key Claims

| # | Relationship | Detail | Certainty |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *P. falciparum* isolates **co-occur with** *pfpm2* increased CNV | Prevalence = **9.8%** in Lubango, southern Angola (hospital; endemic) | Moderate |
| 2 | *pfpm2* increased CNV **resists** piperaquine | Southern Angola; endemic | Moderate |

---

### Clinical Significance

- Prevalence ~10% — low but non-negligible signal in a region where DHA-PPQ may be used
- Piperaquine resistance via *pfpm2* amplification is well-established mechanistically; these isolates represent a sentinel finding
- No quantified effect size for resistance magnitude from this source

---

### Caveats

- Evidence grade: **expert opinion** (observational prevalence study, no experimental resistance confirmation)
- Both claims **pending review**
- CIs not reported — point estimate only
- Sample from single hospital; may not represent regional spread

---

### Bottom Line

~1-in-10 *P. falciparum* isolates in southern Angola carry *pfpm2* CNV amplification — a known piperaquine resistance marker. Low prevalence now, but warrants **molecular surveillance** before DHA-PPQ scale-up in this region.
